The warriors of Aram-Zaneth are known as the Flame-Taken, fearsome berserkers marked by ritual scarification and chosen through visions of divine madness. In battle, they claim to hear the voice of Zaneth himself, urging them into ecstatic fury. Their bodies are latticed with burns, brands, and ash-inked runes—each a mark of devotion and initiation. Clad in copper-wrought armor and reeking of sacred smoke, they fight not merely to kill, but to kindle revelation through violence.
 
Alongside them march diviner-warlocks, enigmatic battlefield seers who wear veils of golden thread that shimmer with protective incantations. These arcane priests wield scrolls and staves inscribed in blood-inked glyphs, which they activate with chants and dream-speech to twist the threads of fate, unravel enemy formations, or ignite the very flesh of their foes. To witness one work a battle-sigil is to watch reality momentarily bend under the weight of forgotten names and sacrificial promises.
 
Aram-Zaneth is no stranger to forbidden alliances. Many among their priesthood and war-castes have formed pacts with rogue Efreet, especially those who still remember the splendor of the Brass City before its fall. These exiled fire-spirits, consumed by bitterness and freed from the strictures of the Brass City, find kinship in the blood-soaked rites of Aram-Zaneth, and often lend their power in exchange for worship, sacrifices, or a promise of eventual reckoning against the city's false heirs.
 
Their cavalry is no less terrifying. Mounted on scaled hounds or desert lizards, bred in sacred birthing pits and trained through torment and fire, these riders strike swiftly from the dunes. Their mounts are more than beasts—they are ritually awakened, possessed by spirits or imprinted with glyphs that make them resistant to fear and pain. A charge of these riders is both a physical and spiritual assault, the thunder of claws and hooves accompanied by the howls of spirits too long shackled in the dark.
 
In every corner of their martial tradition, Aram-Zaneth wields battle not as strategy, but as sacrament—a holy theater in which fate may be rewritten in blood and fire.
