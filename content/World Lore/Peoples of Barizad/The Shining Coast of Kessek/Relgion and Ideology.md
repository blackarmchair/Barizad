The Nisari worship Nimurah, goddess of the mirror-sea, hidden truths, trade, and tides of fate.

- Her temples are chambers of still water and silvered glass, where priests do not preach—they refract.
- Nimurah is depicted as a veiled woman with a face of rippling water, bearing both oaths and secrets.
- Her clergy serves as mediators, contract-witnesses, and consecrators of sealed deals. When invoked in trade, her presence is binding—though often cryptic.
-   
    
 
Nimurah’s Three Tenets:

1. All tides return.
2. Nothing seen is whole.
3. A price paid twice is still paid.
