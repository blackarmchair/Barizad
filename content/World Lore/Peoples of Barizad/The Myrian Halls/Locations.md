Kherum-Zan
 
“_Where the Sky-Prince first touched earth and the stars wrote flame into stone, there rose Kherum-Zan—the root of lineage, the mouth of remembrance._”  
—Opening lines of The Descent Chronicle
 
**Overview**  
Kherum-Zan rests on the central floodplain of the River Mereth, built atop a great natural rise shaped into seven terraced platforms. It is the ritual, scholarly, and mnemonic heart of the Myrian Halls, considered sacred by all city-states in the coalition.￼

- At its highest level stands the _Ziggurat of Naram-Kel_, an immense star-stepped structure of basalt and brass, believed to mark the Sky-Prince’s landing.￼
- The _Chronocrypt_ spirals beneath the ziggurat—an ever-growing archive of brass-thread scrolls, dream-gem fragments, and Burning Tablet shards.￼
- The _Mirror Scriptorium_, perched on the city’s eastern cliff, houses the Lens-Binders—augurs who render celestial judgments by reading mirrored glyphs suspended in fire-oil.￼
- The _Stele Fields_, surrounding Kherum-Zan, are a sacred necropolis of memory obelisks, each one etched with the names and deeds of lineage-keepers and prophet-kings.
 
**Governance and Faith**  
Kherum-Zan is ruled by the sitting Prophet-King of Descent, always a direct descendant of Naram-Kel and custodian of the central Burning Tablet.￼  
**Spiritual Core and Magic**  
Kherum-Zan is not a temple city—it is a celestial mechanism, meticulously aligned with seasonal conjunctions and memory tides.￼

- The Hall of the Sealed Flame holds the first and oldest Burning Tablet, still too hot to touch, interpreted only through shadow and echo.￼
- Magic here is recovered, not invented—with clerics invoking Sky-Born through consecrated masks and wizards tracing ancient predictions rather than improvising spells.
 
**Current Tensions**

- The Flame beneath the First Tablet flickers for the first time in recorded memory—some claim it is the beginning of a new descent, others fear a false return.￼
- A forgotten descendant of Naram-Kel, thought to have died a century ago, has returned bearing a shard of memory not recorded in any lineage.￼
- A splinter group of Lens-Binders has begun inscribing “future tablets”—a heretical act condemned by traditionalists but increasingly supported by younger scholars (possibly under the influence of Aram-Zaneth).￼
- Sirr al-Nar’s agents whisper promises of “a second sky,” hoping to co-opt Myrian hopes for a restored Brass City with their own celestial machine.
