The Myrians do not worship faceless divinities. Their gods were once celestial functionaries, star-guides, or vision-keepers who descended in flame during the age of Zahr al-Narās.
 
Notable Figures:

- Naram-Kel, the Sky-Prince – Founder of the Myrian bloodline; said to have fallen from the city in an ark of brass and memory.￼
- Zarephah, the Veiled Watcher – Oracle of the Third Dawn; blindfolded seer said to still walk the meridians of the world in dream-form.￼
- Thuun, the Oath-Sleeper – Patron of lineage and remembrance, invoked during burial and inheritance rites.￼

Worship is communal, chanted, and recorded, often carved into clay cylinders or wound into metallic scrolls spun from thin brass thread.
