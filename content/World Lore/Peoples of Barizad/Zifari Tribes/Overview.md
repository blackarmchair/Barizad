_Zifari: from the Hebrew zaphar (to whistle, blow), evokes wind, sand, and breath—elements of mystic storytelling and oral tradition._
 
The Zifari are a loose confederation of nomadic desert clans, oral historians, spirit-binders, and dream-chasers. They trace their origins to a time before the Brass City rose, claiming descent from those who sang to the stars before they had names.
 
They do not build permanent cities; their homes are mirage-tents, obsidian-threaded yurts, and bone-framed caravans that vanish like windstorms. The Zifari claim it is not land, but memory and motion that makes a people real.
