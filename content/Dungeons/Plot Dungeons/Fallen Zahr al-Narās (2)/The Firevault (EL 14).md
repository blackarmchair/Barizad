1. **Description**
    
    1. The "Firevault" was originally built to allow the Celestine Furnace to vent heat out of the city.
    2. It still serves this purpose but several of its mechanisms have been damaged.
    3. The initial entry features three oscillating columns, originally meant to convey heat and flame outside, have cracks and blown gaskets (likely damaged in the fall) and now spray blasts of searing holy flame at regular intervals.
    4. The floors of the second chamber were also designed as heat sinks (harmless to the city's Efreeti residents but deadly to mortals) and a rolling wave of fire cascades from E to W across the floor at regular intervals.
        
        1. However, there are several broken tiles that do not eminate fire (also the result of damage); these can serve as "safe spaces" in a pinch￼
2. **Mechanics**
    
    1. **Flaming Pillars**: In order to safely pass the columns a player must first succeed on a Perception check (DC 15) to understand the timing of the fire pillars and then make a successful Acrobatics check (DC 15) to apply that knowledge in a way that lets them pass unscathed.
        
        1. If a player passes both checks they pass on to the hallway without damage.
        2. If they fail either or both checks, sum the total points of difference by which each check was failed (n); the player takes nd6 damage￼
    2. **Flaming Floor**: A series of 6 loud clicks herald the coming of a new wave of flame which proceeds from E to W across the floor over the course of 2 rounds.
        
        1. Any creature caught on a flame square as the flame passes by takes 5d8 fire/holy damage
        2. A Spot check (DC 16) reveals that several squares are non-functional and can be safely stood on as the fire passes￼
    3. **Vault Door**: The door to the vault is locked by conventional means.
        
        1. Its lock can be picked with a successful Open Lock (DC 17) check
        2. It can be broken: Hardness 10, 90hp, Break DC 30￼
3. **Encounters**
    
    1. The second chamber contains a _Fire Giant_ who has taken-up residence in the ruined city and uses this room as a safe haven and uses its vault as storage for various treasure he has stolen (MM 121)
    2. There are also two _Large Fire Elementals_ who found a suitable home in the chamber (MM 99)￼
