Based on: Original concept
 
Dungeon Concept: A wind-blown ruin filled with the voices of lost wanderers. The sands themselves sing riddles and truths, blocking or guiding passage.
 
Dungeon Highlights:

- Dunes whisper puzzles and misdirection
- Players must answer riddles or lose their way
 
Dream-Gem: _The Memory of Wind_ – a song once meant to guide but now to mislead
 
Rooms

1. Entryway
    
    1. Description: The party enters a sandstone-walled room. The walls are high (about 15ft) and the ground has a thin layer of sand blown-in from outside. There are two torches, one on each side wall, that provide light (one wonders if they are magical and never go-out). Opposite the party as they enter, there is a large smooth stone door on the far wall flanked on either side by a decorative obelisk. The room is also filled with a great many crates each open and filled to the brim with a variety of assorted mundate household and agricultural items: rope, candles, plates, taxidermized beasts including a viper, a desert fox, an impressively. Large scarab beetle, a horned lizard, and a carrion bird of some esoteric variety. In the middle of the room, a single leatherbound book rests open on the floor (as if placed there on purpose).￼
    2. The book is open to a page with the following text written in flowing calligraphy:￼_"The key you seek is here somewhere.__￼__Do not be so hasty or look past what's right there.__￼__We all have one.__￼__Some big, some small, some long and tall,__￼__and even when it's stuffed it can run._"
        
        1. The rest of the book is blank pages, it's cover is also blank￼
    3. The answer to the riddle is "your nose".
        
        1. The 1st PC to even suggest this immediately feels a strong impulse to sneeze which soon overpowers them.
        2. Sneezing in this way causes a small key to fly out of the sneezer's nose; this is the key to the door
        3. The crates and everything in them are non-magical red herrings￼
2. The False Desert
    
    1. _Upon passing through the stone door in the Entryway, the party finds themselves in a vast desert expanse—open sky, blazing sun, and rippling dunes. There is no sign of walls or a ceiling, even though this must logically be inside the fragment.__￼__￼__Time feels warped. The sun never moves. The wind never stops. Somewhere, distant voices sing in harmony or agony.__￼_
    2. The PCs can choose to journey through the desert. The only way to find the "exit" is to:
        
        1. First become lost by travelling in any direction away from the entrance for a few hours.
        2. Make the willful decision to back-track to the entrance.
        3. Find that the entrance is no longer the entrance but instead has become the way forward.￼
    3. There are several encounters to be found in the desert; every 1-2 hours of travel one of the following should happen:
        
        1. Whispers in the Dunes: PCs hear voices that sound like old friends or lost loved ones; Will save (DC 16) to resist the urge to run-off alone towards the voices. If a player fails, spring a random encounter on them with a surprise round.
        2. Random Encounter:
            
            1. 1 Belker
            2. 1 Basilisk and 2 Thoqqua
            3. 2d3 Monstrous Scorpions (Large)
            4. 1d4+1 Howlers
            5. 1d4+1 Dust Mephit
            6. 1d6+2 Thoqqua
        3. Mirage Puzzle: PCs arrive at a 4-way paved crossroads in the middle of the desert. Each path has an obelisk beside it with the same inscription:￼"_I am the path you always walk,_
        
        _Though never set in stone._  
        _I start and end where you began,_  
        _Yet leave you far from home._  
        _To find me is to lose your way—_  
        _To leave me, know your own._"￼This is a hint to the PCs to go back the way they came￼
        
3. Gate to Sphinx's Layer
    
    1. Description: The floor of this room is constructed of large flagstones. Seven of these flagstones are carved with individual runic markings; the rest are bare. An engraving in a strange runic script, similar to those on the flagstones themselves, covers much of the west wall.￼
    2. Mechanics: The seven marked flagstones are arranged in no discernable pattern around the room. Each flagstone bears one of the seven smbols from the following group: (see TGBOTPAD for inscription)￼
    3. Each of the flagstones is a pressure plate that activates when at least 30lbs of pressure is applied. The party will feel these pressure plates activate when they step on the flagstones OR a successful Search or Spot check (DC 15) reveals their nature without stepping on them.￼
    4. When the pressure plates are activated in the correct order the door opens. The key to determining the correct order can be found in the inscription on the west wall. It looks like this: (see TGBOTPAD for inscription)￼
    5. If the pressure plates are activated in the incorrect order they reset and a trap is sprung: each player within 10ft of any of the flagstones must make a Reflex save (DC 16) or suffer 20 (5d8) lightning damage on a failed save; half damage on a successful save.￼
4. Sphinx's Layer:
    
    1. Use the ANDROSPHINX from the MM
    2. As the final rune-lock clicks into place, the air shudders. The oppressive hum of the wind dies instantly.￼￼A seam splits down the far wall, and ancient stone grinds open like the yawning jaws of something old and wise. A warm, dry wind escapes the chamber beyond, thick with the scent of incense, dust, and old blood.￼￼Beyond lies a vast, echoing chamber carved from solid sandstone. The walls are etched with forgotten hieroglyphs and half-buried memories—some glowing faintly, others scrubbed away by claw or time.￼￼At its center, upon a raised dais of polished obsidian, reclines the Sphinx. His leonine body sprawls with effortless majesty, wings folded like judgment held in reserve. ￼￼His face—half divine, half monstrous—studies you without blinking.￼￼Around him, the wind swirls slowly, not in circles, but in runes, as if the air itself dares not speak without her permission.￼￼He does not rise. He does not smile.￼￼Instead, he speaks in a voice that does not echo, but seems to already be inside your head:￼“I sing but have no throat.￼I remember, but have no mind.
    
    I build your steps and erase them after.  
    What am I?”
    
      
    5. The answer to this riddle is "the wind" or "the desert itself". If the PCs guess this, their prize is the desert. If they fail to guess it they are attacked. The Sphinx knows the only way out is through him and is just toying with the party.
