**Humans**  
The dominant race of Barizad, humans are as diverse and multifaceted as the lands they inhabit. Every major civilization—be it the theocratic halls of Aram-Zaneth, the Satrapies of Varnash, or the wandering Zifari tribes—is shaped by human ambition, philosophy, and error. Adaptable, driven, and deeply entangled in the broken weave of fate, humans strive to reclaim the dreams scattered by the fall of Zahr al-Narās, whether through conquest, communion, or heresy.
 
**Dwarves**  
Stalwart and obsessive, dwarves once served as stewards and engineers in the auxiliary vaults of the Brass City. Though most of their great forges and libraries were lost in the calamity, some still carry ancient schemata etched into their bones. They are perfectionists in craft and creed, haunted by a half-remembered sense of divine purpose. Many now seek fragments of True Brass, not for power—but to restore their people's forgotten role in fate’s machinery.
 
**Elves**  
Elves are a rare and distant people in Barizad, hailing from a far-off land untouched by the fall of the Brass City. Their long lives and alien customs make them enigmatic figures in local eyes—neither part of the Celestine design nor wholly separate from its influence. Some whisper that the elves’ ancestors were intentionally excluded from the writing of fate, though whether as punishment or preservation is unknown even to them.
 
**Halflings**  
Halflings are found almost exclusively among the Zifari tribes, where they serve as guides, omen-readers, and cunning traders. They are regarded as “slippery” by many fatebound societies, blessed with luck or cursed with exemption from destiny. Halflings move through the world with an intuitive grace, often interpreting the signs others overlook—cracks in a salt pan, the flicker of wind in tent canvas, or the sound of laughter just before a storm.
 
**Half-Elves**  
Caught between the mortal and the ineffable, half-elves carry a spiritual dissonance that marks them as outsiders even among their kin. Many are drawn to the heresies of Aram-Zaneth or wander as dream-seekers, interpreters, or scholars of lost prophecy. Their blood is both a blessing and a burden—proof of union, but also of contradiction—and the gears of fate grind uncomfortably around them.
 
**Half-orcs**  
Half-orcs in Barizad are most often born of violence—products of border raids, punitive campaigns, or cycles of captivity between orcish warbands and the dominant human nations. Orcs, when captured, are frequently enslaved by satrapies or frontier provinces, used as labor or shock troops in conflicts not their own. Half-orcs arise from these grim exchanges, and their presence is a source of discomfort, shame, or suspicion in nearly every culture.
 
Few are afforded dignity. Many grow up as outcasts, pressed into servitude or forced to prove their worth tenfold. Yet those who survive often possess remarkable resilience, keen instincts, and a burning desire to become more than what others assume of them. In a world obsessed with fate and order, half-orcs are unchosen children of consequence—and they carry that weight with unbreakable grit.
