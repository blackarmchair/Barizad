## Faction Escalation Guide

### Early Game (Act I)

Factions compete quietly. Agents make offers, spies observe, scouts map fragment locations. Violence is rare -- everyone is still assessing the situation.

**Signs the PCs should notice:**
- Faction representatives arriving in settlements
- Competing offers for gem custody
- Rumors of other expeditions

### Mid Game (Act II)

Factions send full expeditions. Dungeons become contested ground. The PCs arrive at fragments to find them already breached or must race rivals.

**Signs the PCs should notice:**
- Looted dungeons
- NPC contacts pressured or threatened by other factions
- Open skirmishes between faction agents

### Late Game (Act III)

Factions wage open war for fragments. Alliances form and shatter. Sirr al-Nar acts directly.

**Signs the PCs should notice:**
- Settlements choosing sides
- Former allies making ultimatums
- The sky changing over regions where gems have been activated

## Session Loop

The core gameplay loop after the first fragment:

1. **Rumor** -- the PCs hear about a new fragment, faction activity, or anomaly
2. **Travel** -- journey to the region, encountering the world's current state
3. **Faction interference** -- someone else wants what's in the fragment, or wants to stop the PCs
4. **Fragment dungeon** -- the exploration/puzzle/combat core
5. **Dream-Gem recovered** -- a new gem with new stakes
6. **World consequence** -- reality shifts based on what the PCs do with the gem

This loop is semi-episodic but cumulative. Each cycle raises the stakes and narrows the options.
