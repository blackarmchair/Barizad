The Shattering of Destiny  
When Zahr al-Narās fell, it ruptured more than stone and sky—it shattered the Celestine Furnace, the divine engine that once burned mortal futures into ordained truth. That collapse tore open the veil between what _was meant to be_ and _what could be_, flooding the world with a storm of unstable possibility.
 
Now, the fabric of reality quivers beneath the strain of too many futures vying for dominance. In places where fragments of the Brass City still linger, entire regions bend or fracture under the influence of “true dreams”—residual shards of fate encoded in luminous prophecy-gems. These gems do not merely predict. They create.
 
The War for the Future  
In the wake of the Furnace’s failure, nearly every major faction has turned its eyes toward the true dreams, each seeking to shape the future in its image.

- The Aram-Zaneth believe the time has come to rewrite reality itself. They aim to weaponize or distort the true dreams, replacing divine design with their brand of ideological heresy—a cosmos born not from order, but from defiance.
- Sirr al-Nar, the fallen Harmonist, sees the gems as tools to rebuild Zahr al-Narās under his control. He feeds the Furnace new dreams—ones that elevate his design and suppress all deviation.
- The Emerald Courts seek restoration, hoping the gems can revive their star-priest dynasties and ensure that ancestral sovereignty passes unbroken into the next age.
- The Satrapies of Varnash approach the gems with fear and ambition. Some nobles wish to shatter or hoard the fragments, ensuring fate’s restoration cannot erode their hard-won power.
- The Myrian Halls adopt a more cautious stance. They aim to study and catalog each gem exhaustively before committing to any metaphysical reshaping of the world.
- The Nisarun, true to form, offer to broker neutrality. Yet their wind-ships are often seen transporting dreams to multiple buyers, playing every side while pledging loyalty to none.
- Among the Zifari, there is no consensus—but their seers and wind-callers agree on one truth: fate should not be restored. They act to preserve plural futures, ensuring no single vision dominates the desert horizon.
