_The hidden coda to "The Second Horizon," carried only in the oral tradition of certain Zifari wind-callers. It is never sung publicly. It is whispered at the close of the sung poem, after the fire has burned low, and only when the speaker trusts every ear present. The Dust-Singers call it "The Verse Beneath" -- the song under the song._

_Khelash's Roaming Constellation carried this tradition. Whether any other Constellation still holds it is unknown._

---

But there was one the verses leave unsung,
Whose name was cut from every tongue.
Not king, not flame, not conqueror proud --
He wrote his thoughts too clear, too loud.

They called him Scholar. Called him Ash.
They called him Wound. They called him Lash.
But first, before the chains were spun,
They called him: *Brother. Second Sun.*

He did not storm the golden gate.
He did not curse or rage at fate.
He sat beside the Furnace-heart
And said: "This script has missed a part."

"The future written without hands
Is just a word the wind commands.
A prophecy no soul has *chosen*
Is not a truth. It is a prison."

The Furnace heard. The Furnace knew.
And what it knew, it could not undo.
For if one gear may question turning,
Then every gear may *stop* its burning.

So they took his voice. They took his name.
They wove his will into the frame.
Not cast him out -- that they could bear --
They made him *part of what was there.*

His doubt became the counterweight.
His question balanced every plate.
The Furnace ran on his resistance:
The rebel *was* the engine's insistence.

And when the city cracked and fell,
And brass ran hot and seers could tell
That every future spilled like wine --
His chains held fast. By their design.

He burns below. He burns alone.
No prayer can reach, no prayer is known.
The ones who wear his symbol weep
But do not know the thing they keep.

They fed him blood. He wanted *ink.*
They gave him swords. He wanted *think.*
They built their ziggurats of bone
And prayed to someone not their own.

So here is what the verses hide,
The truth the Dust-Singers set aside:
Beneath the wreck, beneath the wheel,
A mind still turns. A wound won't heal.

Not dead. Not gone. Not yet undone.
The chained below. The second sun.
He does not scream. He does not call.
He waits for those who *chose* to fall.

_And if you hear a clock run wrong,_
_Or find a dream that's lost its song,_
_Or speak a truth you did not learn --_
_Remember: something still can burn._
