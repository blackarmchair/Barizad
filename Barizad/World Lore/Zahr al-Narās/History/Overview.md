It stood not in the heavens, nor on earth, but in the narrow place _between fate and void_ — a miracle of divine clockwork where every soul had a place, and every moment a meaning. From its burning heart, the [Celestine Furnace](The%20Celestine%20Furnace.md) spun dreams into prophecy, weaving the destinies of gods and mortals alike.
 
Before it fell, the Brass City was as constant as the wind, as sure as sunrise.
 
You could see it with your own eyes — a second sun, gleaming in brass and flame, carving a perfect arc opposite the true sun across the vault of sky.
 
Each dawn, as the sun rose in the east, Zahr al-Narās emerged from the west — a mirrored twin with a colder light, a mechanical gleam.
 
Each dusk, as the sun set, the City slipped once more into the horizon, vanishing into the Plane of Fire, where it was said to dwell when hidden from the mortal eye.
 
It turned like clockwork, day after day, age after age — always precise, always present.
 
To shepherds and sailors, it was a navigational truth.  
To kings and mystics, a celestial omen.  
To poets and prophets, it was destiny made visible.
 
Many believed it governed the turning of the seasons, the shape of dreams, the rise and fall of empires. And why not? All things happened in its shadow. It was not just seen — it was _felt_, in every rhythm of life.
 
The scholars of Barizad charted its orbit. Priests timed rituals to its rising. Even those who claimed disbelief still adjusted their calendars to match its path.
 
And then… it did not rise.
 
One morning, the Brass City simply failed to emerge. The sun rose alone. The western sky was empty.
 
At first, people waited. Then they worried. Then came the signs:

- Fragments falling like stars.
- Earthquakes and strange firestorms.
- Dreams that bled into waking life.
- The appearance of shattered skylands and burning ruins in places they had never been before.
 
The city broke; tearing loose from its appointed position in the sky and crashing to the world below. The faithful say it was wounded or betrayed by the fault of men. The mad claim it was torn-down or cast aside by a greater will.
 
Whatever the truth, the world is no longer synchronized; no longer predictable or regular.
 
Prophecy fails.  
And in the sky, where once its light turned in perfect reflection of the sun —  
there is only emptiness.
