**“Fed with star-blood and bound oaths, it boiled futures like steam from a god’s mouth.”**
 
Deep within the core of Zahr al-Narās — past the flame-wound halls, beneath the sapphire domes that tracked the movements of fate — there stood the Celestine Furnace.
 
It was no mere engine, though it turned.  
No simple shrine, though it radiated mystical energy.  
It was a machine of impossible breadth, a living crucible of brass, crystal, and bound flame — and it was the center around which the Brass City turned.
 
The old texts say it was built to refine the future, or perhaps to anchor it.  
Others call it the _Font of Prophecy_, the _Womb of Time_, or the _Living Clock_.  
None of these names are without merit.
 
What is known — what is whispered even now — is this:

- The Celestine Furnace was fed with dreams. Not ordinary ones, but the _true_ dreams — the raw, volatile visions of seers, mystics, and chosen vessels.
- Each was captured and stored in a jeweled crucible: a gem cut to hold destiny.
- These were fed to the Furnace in solemn procession.
- And in return, the world moved forward with _purpose_.

It is said the Furnace did not simply see the future — it shaped it, distilling infinite possibility into a single, burning thread. A thread that the City followed, and through it, the world.
 
The mechanism itself defies sense. Clockwork beyond comprehension: gears the size of cathedrals, orbs of polished aether spinning in suspended gyres, incantation-loops etched in molten brass and chanted by the intricate bellows of the machine itself. Choirs of bound fire-scribes once maintained its rituals. No mortal hands ever touched its workings — only watched, and feared, and obeyed.
 
And at the center of it all — buried deeper than even the Furnace’s architects could reach — something beat.
 
Not a heart of flesh, but of liquid foresight, molten and alive. It pulsed with every spoken prophecy. It flared with every diverted fate. It may have been a soul. It may have been a god. It may have been a question that no one dared ask.
 
Now, the Furnace lies broken, its crucibles scattered across the world. Some shattered, some still full, some whispering.
 
And without it, time is unwound thread, tangled and fraying.
 
What remains burns cold.  
What was true may no longer be.  
And the heart… the heart may still beat.

## The Origin of the Dream-Gems

The Furnace once distilled infinite possibility into a single, burning thread of prophecy. But Zaneth's rebellion fractured that mechanism before the City fell. His act introduced a crack in deterministic fate -- and when the Furnace finally shattered, the universe suddenly contained **multiple unrealized futures** that could no longer be collapsed into one.

Those futures needed a way to persist without destroying the timeline entirely. They condensed into crystallized possibilities -- the prophecy-gems.

The gems are not, strictly speaking, Zaneth's dreams. They are **human dreams that became possible because of him**. Before his rebellion, there was only one future. After it, there were many. When the Furnace broke, those many futures had nowhere to go. They solidified into the gems the world now fights over.

In other words: Zaneth did not create the gems. He created a universe where they could exist.

This means the entire campaign -- every fragment dungeon, every faction scrambling for gems, every future murdered or preserved -- unfolds within the space that Zaneth's rebellion opened. The PCs are navigating the consequences of his original act, whether they know it or not.

The Aram-Zaneth would view the gems as something close to sacred metaphysical artifacts -- not relics to worship, but proof of their theology. Each gem demonstrates that destiny is not singular, the future is plural, and mortal will can select among possibilities. That is Zanethi doctrine in physical form.

Zaneth himself, if he cares about the gems at all, likely regards them as unresolved experiments -- interesting but not precious. Side effects of his refusal to accept imposed order.
