Before the fall, even gods were bound to the will of Zahr al-Narās.
 
They did not rule fate—they _served_ it.  
Each divine act, each answered prayer, each miracle: a stroke along the preordained wheel, measured and permitted by the hidden workings of the Brass City.
 
But one among resented his subjugation.  
One among them asked: _What if my will could turn the wheel?_
 
His name was Zaneth — Scholar of Ashes. The First Blasphemer.
 
He was not a rebel in the mortal sense. He did not raise armies or burn temples.  
His crime was conceptual.
 
He believed fate could be _edited_—_not_ defied in ignorance or passion, but remade, thoughtfully, carefully, with full understanding of the consequences.
 
Zaneth sought to forge new destinies within the very architecture of the City. Not to break the system, but to rewrite it from the inside — a divine act of authorship.
 
To most, he was not evil.  
To the architects of the Brass City, he was incompatible — a contradiction in motion.  
His prayers no longer echoed correctly.  
His miracles rang off-tempo.  
The Celestine Furnace rejected his offerings.
 
So they chained him.
 
Not in hatred; but in patient, quiet necessity.  
Not as punishment, but as quarantine.
 
He was bound in fire and silence. His name excised from liturgies, his glyphs struck from records. His followers — those few who still listened — whisper his teachings not as prophecy, but as _counterpoint_.
 
They say he still burns, somewhere deep beneath the ruins, a divine ember held in stasis.  
They say he speaks through broken clocks and dreams that forget how they end.  
They say the fall of the City was not his doing — but his chance.
 
For in a world where fate is cracked and bleeding, Zaneth’s vision might finally take root.
 
A future not inherited, but chosen.
A world no longer read from the scroll of time, but written by trembling hand.

## The Dreamer and the Stories (DM Note)

There is a deep structural parallel here with *The Thousand and One Nights*. Zaneth is chained away from Barizad -- "sleeping" in the Fundament. His act of shattering prophecy made multiple futures possible. Those futures crystallized as Dream-Gems.

In the logic of the *Nights*, stories told at night hold back the king's execution. In Barizad, dreams hold back the consummation of fate. Zaneth is the cosmic dreamer whose rebellion prevents the universe from closing into a single ordained ending.

If freed, Zaneth walks into the Furnace -- the dreamer entering the fire where stories are forged into reality. The dreams the party gathered become the raw material of the new cosmos. Instead of fate being finalized, the universe remains a story still being told.
