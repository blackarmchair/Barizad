The Zifari follow a non-doctrinal belief system centered around story, motion, and the living desert. They do not worship gods in a traditional sense. Instead, they venerate spirits, memories, and ancestral dreams that live in the wind, stone, and fire.
 
- Dreams are alive, and every person must carry one to the end of the world or risk being forgotten by the stars.
- The fall of the Brass City is seen as a fracture in the World-Song, a holy silence into which they now sing their own verse.
- Prophets are often called Wind-Dreamers, people who can “hear” the hidden voices carried by the wind.

They reject the fate-scripted determinism of the Celestine Order and view the Aram-Zaneth as corrupted mirrors—those who chained their dreams instead of following them.
