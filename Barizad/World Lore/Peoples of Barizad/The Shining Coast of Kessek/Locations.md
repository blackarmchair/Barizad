Tirazûn
 
**Other Names**  
Tirazûn roughly means _“Record Across Waters”_ in Old Nisari trade-cant. Among merchants, it’s called The Deep Ledger. Among rivals: _The City of Sails and Lies._
   

**Overview**  
Tirazûn stands in the bay of an archipelago at the mouth of the great sea. It is not a city in the traditional sense, but a woven flotilla of anchored reef-halls, brass-anchored ledger spires, and floating bazaars.

- _Concord Spire_ rises from the city center: a sea-glass and brass tower where the Concord of Keels meets beneath the watchful seal of Nimurah.
- The _Salt Vaults_ beneath the spire house the magical ledger-seals—oath-bound tokens that shimmer with layers of debt, favor, and prophecy.
- The _Gossamer Span_, a colossal bridge of knotted silver-thread and sailcloth, connects the inner structures and drifts to the mainland.
- Tide-Court Galleons, permanent moored ships now serving as courts, brothels, or arbitration chambers, line the _Whisper Docks_.
 
**City Culture**  
Tirazûn is the soul of Nisarun’s economy: nothing is free and everything is for sale.

- Daily life is governed by the _Fluctuating Tide_, a market bell rung in intervals dictated by dream-diviners tracking astral tides.
- Ship-titles outweigh land titles; the _Port-Right Registry_ determines legal jurisdiction based on which vessel one last swore fealty aboard.
- Public contracts are held in the _Chamber of Echoed Oaths_, where disembodied dream-voices recite the terms to assembled witnesses.
 
**Temples**  
The greatest temple to Nimurah—the Hall of Still Waters—rests at the pinnacle of the _Concord Spire_.

- Traders enter barefoot, walking on rippling light as the priesthood offers no sermons, only silence.
- Here, Sea-Veils—priest-seers—refuse questions, but answer through choreographed gesture, refraction mirrors, and ink dropped into still pools.
