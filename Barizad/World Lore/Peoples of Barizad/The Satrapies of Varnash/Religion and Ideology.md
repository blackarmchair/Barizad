The Varnathi pantheon is structured as a celestial court, mirroring the empire’s own social order. Its gods are dignified, ambitious, and hierarchical, and most temples double as schools of rhetoric, art, and statecraft. Worship is public, ornamental, and unabashedly proud.
 
Zirad, Sovereign of Gleam and Inheritance  
_High King of the Divine Court_

- Patron of rulers, legacies, and glorious self-possession
- Depicted wearing a crown of polished brass that reflects every viewer's own image
- Worshipped primarily by nobles, satraps, and anyone claiming divine right
 
Nahara, The Forge-Mother  
_Goddess of Shaping, True Brass, and Artifice_

- Guardian of smiths, engineers, and tradition
- Said to have forged the first star-metal from the bones of extinct suns
- True Brass is her blood made solid; her temples serve as both foundries and shrines
- Common among artisan guilds, armorsmiths, and firebound scholars
 
Virelune, Mirror of Graces  
_Goddess of Beauty, Duel, and Public Glory_

- Patron of dancers, duelists, politicians, and performers
- Believed to reflect the divine in every mortal act of elegance or defiance
- Her rituals involve mirrors, choreographed movement, and silvered ink
- Favors boldness, charm, and victory through flair
 
Lesser Gods and Household Spirits  
The pantheon contains dozens of lesser divinities, including:

- Kavreth, the Coinbinder – god of trade and elegant theft
- Senna-of-the-Drapes – goddess of silk, gossip, and hidden truth
- Varnu-Taz, the Watchful Flame – minor protector spirit invoked before travel or duels
- Children of Nahara – minor deities said to inhabit specific veins of True Brass
