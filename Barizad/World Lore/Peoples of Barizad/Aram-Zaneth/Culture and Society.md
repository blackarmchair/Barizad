The society of Aram-Zaneth is deeply clan-based, with each clan maintaining its own bloodline traditions, ancestral rites, and regional customs. Yet above them all presides the Council of Thorns—a secretive and revered conclave of oracular priests and seers. These figures interpret the will of Zaneth and the Thirsting Court through ancient divination rites, reading omens in ash patterns, sacrificial entrails, and the fevered visions pulled from communal dreams. Their word is law.
 
Slavery is a grim but accepted part of life in Aram-Zaneth. Foreign captives, often taken in raids or purchased from desert traders, are not merely laborers—they are ritual fuel. Some are sacrificed in blood rites to power dream-binding ceremonies, their essence offered to Zaneth in exchange for prophetic visions. Others are bartered to Djinn warlords and exiled spirits, traded for fragments of forbidden lore or whispered truths from the age before the Brass City ruled the skies.
 
Their artisans are not mere craftsmen, but ritual smiths and curse-binders, fusing spiritual malice into every item they create. Weapons of copper and obsidian—cold to the touch, yet humming with malignant energy—are common. They also produce fetishes carved from bone and ashwood, and idol-totems of twisted design, often worn or placed at shrines. These objects are not simply sacred—they are alive with spiritual force, some whispering in the dark, others burning with silent hatred for all things that walk under the open sky.
 
To outsiders, Aram-Zaneth may seem cruel and otherworldly, but to its people, every act is a sacrament, every cruelty a necessary toll paid to glimpse the truths hidden beneath the world’s skin.
