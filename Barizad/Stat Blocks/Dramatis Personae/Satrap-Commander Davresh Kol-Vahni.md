## Personality

Davresh is the youngest satrap-commander in three generations, appointed not through bloodline (though his is respectable) but through an unbroken record of victories against Aram-Zaneth border incursions. He is handsome, meticulously groomed, and radiates the particular Varnathi confidence that other cultures find either magnetic or insufferable.

But Davresh is not a fool. Behind the swagger is a man who has seen what the prophecy-gems do to people -- soldiers under his command who woke up with different memories, a border fort that existed on Tuesday and didn't on Thursday. He covers his fear with bravado because that is what Varnash demands, but in private moments, he drinks too much and stares at his True Brass blade as if waiting for it to betray him.

He genuinely believes Varnash is the only power stable enough to safeguard the gems. He is wrong about this, but not unreasonably so.

## Motivation

Davresh has orders from the Shahenshar to secure all Brass City fragments within Varnathi territory -- and to "extend" that territory as needed. He also has a personal stake: his elder brother vanished during a gem-related anomaly. Davresh doesn't know if he was killed, overwritten, or never existed. He wants answers, but he'll follow orders first.

## Relationship to the PCs

Davresh shows up with a patrol after the PCs' first dungeon and *politely* demands the gem under Varnathi sovereign authority. If the PCs push back, he respects strength -- a duel of gleam (or even just a good argument) earns his grudging respect. He becomes a reliable contact if the PCs work with Varnash, or a persistent (but honorable) antagonist if they don't.

He will never backstab the PCs. He will, however, outmaneuver them politically if they're not careful.

## Hooks

- His missing brother is a concrete, personal mystery the PCs can investigate -- and the answer ties into the overwriting mechanic
- He can grant or deny access to Varnathi territory, which contains at least one Fragment Dungeon
- If the PCs earn his trust, he confides that some Varnathi nobles are hoarding gems for personal power, not state security -- potential internal conflict
- In Act III, he must choose between his Shahenshar's orders and what he's learned from the PCs
