## Personality

Naqqara was once the most promising dream-caster in Asmarat's Nemet Spires -- a prodigy who could read the dead's last visions with perfect clarity. Then the visions started contradicting themselves. The embalmed dead began returning from the tomb-canals *unaligned*, their dream-flasks empty. She reported this faithfully. The Court of Embalmed Radiance told her to stop looking.

She didn't stop.

Now she speaks for the Cult of the Hollow Star -- not as a fanatic, but as a woman who followed the evidence where the traditionalists refused to go. She believes the Mirror Falcon has gone blind (or been replaced), and that the Emerald Courts must seek new sources of celestial truth or watch their entire afterlife infrastructure collapse.

She is sharp-tongued, exhausted, and deeply sincere. She does not enjoy being a heretic. She became one because no one else would say what she could see.

## Motivation

Naqqara wants the prophecy-gems because the Emerald Courts' soul-alignment rituals are failing. She believes a true dream, properly interpreted, could recalibrate the Courts' entire funerary cosmology -- or reveal that it was always built on a lie. She is terrified of both outcomes.

## Relationship to the PCs

She approaches the PCs not with demands but with questions. She wants to *study* a gem alongside them, not confiscate it. If the PCs let her examine one, she becomes a loyal (if anxious) ally. If they refuse, she respects it -- but may resort to desperate measures later as the crisis in Asmarat worsens.

She is the NPC most likely to be *overwritten* in Act II. If a dream is consumed that erases the Hollow Star movement, Naqqara either ceases to exist or becomes a loyal Court of Embalmed Radiance traditionalist who doesn't recognize the PCs. This should hurt.

## Hooks

- Can serve as a guide to Emerald Courts politics and the afterlife crisis in Asmarat
- Her Tomb-Speaker abilities let her detect when reality has been rewritten (useful exposition tool)
- If the PCs pursue the Zaneth liberation path, her research on "blind gods" becomes directly relevant
- The God-Queen Seraphae VI wants her silenced; this can become a side quest
