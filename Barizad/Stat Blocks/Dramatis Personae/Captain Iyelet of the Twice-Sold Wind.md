## Personality

Iyelet is the most likable person who will absolutely betray the PCs if the price is right. She is warm, funny, generous with wine, and constitutionally incapable of loyalty to anyone who isn't currently useful. She doesn't see this as a flaw. In Nisarun, reliability is a commodity you purchase, not a virtue you expect for free.

She captains the *Twice-Sold Wind*, a fast brine-runner that operates on the margins of legal trade. She has sold dream-gem fragments to three different factions in the same week and considers this excellent business practice. She is not malicious -- she genuinely likes people, including the PCs -- but she lives in a world where sentiment is a luxury and leverage is survival.

The one crack in her armor: she had a younger sister, Mevra, who was a wind-seer. Mevra was hired to handle a prophecy-gem for a Varnathi noble and hasn't been seen since. Iyelet doesn't talk about this. She drinks about it.

## Motivation

Officially, Iyelet represents the Concord of Keels' interest in dream-gem trade. She wants to buy, broker, or at minimum catalog every gem that surfaces. Unofficially, she's looking for Mevra -- and she suspects a prophecy-gem overwrote her sister out of existence. She needs to know if that's reversible.

## Relationship to the PCs

Iyelet approaches the PCs with an offer: she'll transport them anywhere, provide supplies, share intelligence, and connect them with buyers -- for a cut of whatever they find. She is the most immediately *useful* faction contact, and the least trustworthy.

If the PCs catch her selling information to their enemies, she's not ashamed. "I sold your enemies information about you. I also sold you information about your enemies. Everyone's informed. You're welcome."

If the PCs help her find out what happened to Mevra, her loyalty becomes real -- and that scares her.

## Hooks

- She is the party's potential transport, fence, and intelligence network -- making her indispensable and dangerous
- Her sister Mevra's disappearance is a personal quest that connects to the overwriting mechanic
- She knows things about every faction (because she's sold to all of them) and can provide exposition through gossip
- In Act II, the Concord of Keels may order her to seize a gem by force; this tests whether her relationship with the PCs means more than her career
- She has contacts among the efreeti warlords -- specifically Sul Nashir of the Nine Flames, who hired her for transport once. This is a dangerous thread to pull.
