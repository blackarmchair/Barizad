## Personality

Tahira is the Myrian Halls' answer to the crisis: methodical, patient, and deeply uncomfortable with how fast everything is moving. She was trained to spend decades studying a single ancestral tablet. Now she's in the field cataloging artifacts that rewrite history in real time, and she has not slept well in months.

She speaks precisely, qualifies every statement, and gets visibly distressed when people make decisions about prophecy-gems without fully understanding them. She is not timid -- she has a quiet, immovable stubbornness when she believes she's right. She simply believes that *understanding must precede action*, and the world is currently doing things in the wrong order.

She has a dry, accidental humor -- she states terrifying metaphysical observations in the same tone she'd use to note a citation error.

## Motivation

The Circle of Celestial Descent sent Tahira to study the gems in the field because she is expendable enough to risk and talented enough to be useful. She knows this, and it stings. She wants to prove that the Myrian approach -- careful study, ancestral wisdom, measured response -- is the right one. She also genuinely fears what happens if someone feeds the wrong dream to the Furnace.

Privately, she has begun to suspect that the Myrian bloodline's connection to the Sky-Born may itself be a *written* fate -- something the Furnace created, not something that was ever naturally true. She has told no one.

## Relationship to the PCs

Tahira arrives after the PCs' first or second dungeon, sent by the Myrian Halls to request a gem for study. She is the most reasonable faction contact -- she doesn't demand, threaten, or bribe. She asks, explains her reasoning, and offers her expertise in return.

If the PCs allow her to travel with them, she becomes an invaluable resource: she can identify fate-related effects, warn them about dungeon hazards, and provide exposition about the Celestine Furnace's history. She is not a combatant and knows it.

If a prophecy-gem overwrites reality in her presence, she is one of the few people (besides the PCs) who can *tell*. Her reaction -- quiet horror, furious note-taking -- should be a barometer for how wrong things have gotten.

## Hooks

- Her Chronicle Sight ability makes her a walking exposition tool who can confirm or deny what's been rewritten
- Her secret suspicion about the Myrian bloodline is a bomb: if it comes out, it could shatter the Myrian Halls' entire identity
- She is physically fragile and morally important -- protecting her creates tension in dangerous dungeons
- In Act III, the Circle of Celestial Descent may order her to stop helping the PCs and return the data she's gathered. She must choose between obedience and conscience.
