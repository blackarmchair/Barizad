## Personality

Khelash is old -- not ancient, but weathered in a way that suggests he has been tired for a very long time. He speaks slowly, chooses his words with the care of someone who knows that words shape reality (literally, in this world). He has a habit of pausing mid-sentence, listening to something no one else can hear, then continuing as if nothing happened.

He is the last living member of his Roaming Constellation. He doesn't know if they died or were overwritten. He found himself alone in the desert one morning with memories of forty people that no one else shares. His falcon, Twice, is the only other being that remembers them -- because he named her before and after they disappeared.

Despite this, Khelash is not bitter. He is sad, gentle, and absolutely immovable on one point: **no one should have the power to decide which futures live and which die.** He doesn't oppose the PCs -- he warns them. Every gem they use is a family like his, unmade.

## Motivation

Khelash wants to preserve plural futures. He doesn't want to restore the Furnace, destroy it, or seize it. He wants to ensure that no single vision of the future dominates. The Zifari have always believed that the world is a song with many verses, and Khelash has *personally experienced* what happens when a verse is silenced.

If the Zaneth liberation path exists, Khelash is its natural philosophical ally -- a mutable fate, where prophecy is suggestion rather than decree, is the closest thing to the Zifari worldview.

## Relationship to the PCs

Khelash finds the PCs. He doesn't demand anything. He sits at their fire, shares food, and tells them a story about his Roaming Constellation. Then he says: "Every gem you feed to a cause is a people you unmake. I am not telling you what to do. I am telling you what it costs."

He stays nearby -- not as a party member, but as a recurring presence. He appears when the PCs face moral crossroads. He is never judgmental, only honest. If the PCs make ruthless choices, he becomes quieter and sadder. If they make compassionate ones, he opens up.

He is the NPC who makes the players *feel* the consequences of their choices.

## Hooks

- His Wind Shrine ability can temporarily restore overwritten memories -- allowing "deleted" NPCs to briefly reappear, which is devastating and narratively powerful
- His lost Roaming Constellation is a mystery: were they overwritten, or are they trapped in a Fragment Dungeon?
- He knows the deep desert better than anyone and can guide the PCs to hidden dungeons
- If the PCs pursue the Zaneth path, Khelash is the first to support it -- but he warns them that even Zaneth's vision is still *one being's* idea of freedom
- His falcon Twice is a small, constant reminder that reality has already been broken
