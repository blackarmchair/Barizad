## Physical Layout
- A 30-ft wide domed chamber with no floor — instead, there’s a drop into a glowing abyss of failed timelines 
- A crumbling catwalk winds through the space, with scroll-clusters and memory-plaques hanging overhead like chandeliers 
- Wisps of reversed flame drift upward instead of downward — inverted firelight

## Description
> The room opens into nothing. A yawning chasm stretches below, yet gravity remains — the catwalk beneath your feet is firm, but your eyes scream that you’re walking upside down. Shelves of bronze and glass hang from the ceiling above, filled with scrolls that twitch and unfurl like they’re dreaming.
> 
> Memory echoes whisper across the gulf. Some speak of victories that never happened. Others describe your deaths — not yet real, but not quite false.

## Mechanics
Players may choose to reach up and pluck a memory from the shelves above. Each PC can interact once per visit. Doing so can grant a boon, a revelation… or corruption.

| d6  | Memory Content                                                    | Effect                                                                 |
| --- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | A war you never fought — but clearly lost                         | –2 penalty to Will saves for 24 hours                                  |
| 2   | A lover’s face, burned into your thoughts — you’ve never met them | No mechanical effect; haunting dream next long rest                    |
| 3   | Your own hand, older, signing an ancient treaty                   | +2 to a future Diplomacy or Sense Motive check (banked once)           |
| 4   | Sirr al-Nar weeping in a forgotten corridor                       | Gain +1 to saves vs. fear and compulsion for the next 24 hours         |
| 5   | The moment the city fell — but from above                         | +2 insight bonus on next Knowledge (arcana/history) or initiative roll |
| 6   | The death of someone you don’t know… but mourn                    | Next Will save auto-fails                                              |

### Lore Hooks in the Archive
Players who ask about:
- The Celestine Furnace learn it was not merely an engine — it was a judge of futures, and rejecting its rulings was punishable by erasure.  
- Sirr al-Nar's divergence left gaps in many prophecies. Some scrolls end mid-line, twitching as if waiting to be completed.       
- The Furnace never predicted the players. They are a temporal anomaly, and the city doesn’t know what to make of them.
