## Room Layout
- Suspended in the shattered heart of Zahr al-Narās
- The Forge is a floating platform over a glowing well of molten brass and fractured time-scripts
- Six radial walkways arc out from the center like spokes, broken at the ends — fragments of potential futures, never realized

## Flavor Text (Player-Facing)

> You step onto the platform, and time stutters.
> 
> For a heartbeat, you see a dozen futures splinter: yourself dying, ruling, fleeing, forgotten.
> 
> Then it is just you, and him.
> 
> Sirr al-Nar floats above the forge, robed in flame and brass circuitry, his form phasing slightly in and out of alignment — like a soul stretched between destinies.
> 
> “You have come to finish a story I refused to tell,” he says.
> 
> “Will you add your lines? Or will you speak the final one?”

## Mechanics
Sirr al-Nar makes an offer to the players (if not attacked immediately). He does not try to deceive — he’s past that. He makes 2 offers to the players:

- A place in his new design ("You will be fixed points in the machine to come.")
- A plea to abandon prophecy entirely and become unknowable — to "step outside the script"

### If the PCs refuse Sirr al-Nar
Combat begins

### If the PCs accept one of Sirr al-Nar's Offers
Here are the consequences of acceptance for each of Sirr al-Nar's offers:

#### The Architects of a New Fate

Offer: “Stand with me. You shall become fixed stars in the new machine.”

The players accept Sirr al-Nar’s final proposal: to become his agents or governors in a rebuilt timeline.

###### Ending:
- The campaign ends with the collapse of the present into a rewritten future
- The PCs retain full agency, but exist within Sirr’s design: they govern cities, hold impossible knowledge, enforce destiny
- They slowly realize that free will around them is dissolving
- Final scene: a future historian reads from a scroll the PCs haven’t written yet — but will

This is a "dark gift" ending — the party becomes something like godlings, but at the cost of moral clarity and perhaps identity.

### The Living Flame
Offer: “Abandon your names. Burn your past. Walk with me beyond prophecy.”

Sirr al-Nar offers them immortality — not as rulers, but as timeless spirits outside the script of history.

###### Ending:

- The PCs step into the Celestine Forge and are unwritten
- They become echoes, guardian spirits, or gods in the new reality — known only in dreams or forbidden texts
- Their player characters are no longer people, but concepts — the Spark of Rebellion, the Knife at Destiny’s Throat, etc.
- Final scene: a dying prophet glimpses them in a dream and smiles — because “they did not vanish… they became necessary.”

This ending works beautifully for poetic, high-concept campaigns. It echoes mythic rebirth — they are no longer PCs, but eternal ideas.
