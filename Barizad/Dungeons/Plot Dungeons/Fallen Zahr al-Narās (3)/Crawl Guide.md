# Fallen Zahr al-Narās (Floor 3) — Crawl Guide
*Levels 15–18 | EL 2–18 | Party of Four*

## General Features

**Construction Material.** Fractured metaphysics given form — brass, obsidian, dark glass, and crystalline matrices. The architecture here was never meant for habitation; it was the operating theatre of a fate engine. Walls are smooth where intact but cracked along seams that bleed faint light. Floors are polished obsidian veined with gold. Some surfaces phase in and out of solidity — a wall that is brass one stretch may be glass the next. The deeper the party goes, the less the space obeys physical rules.

**Ceiling Height.** Variable and unreliable. The Threshold of the Threadbare has standard 15-foot ceilings but the doors flicker in and out of position. The Inverted Archive has no floor — a catwalk over a glowing abyss with shelves hanging from above. The Prison of Forgotten Sentences is 3 stories high (60+ feet). The Chamber of Sundered Designs is a hemispherical dome. The Ember Sarcophagus is an 80-foot-diameter vault. The Celestine Forge is an open platform suspended over infinity.

**Lighting.** No natural light. The space itself glows — fractured prophecy leaking through cracks in reality. Treat as dim light (20% miss chance, -2 Spot) with patches of bright light near active fate-script and total darkness in dead zones. The Inverted Archive has inverted flame (fire drifting upward). The Ember Sarcophagus refracts dream-light from orbiting gem shards. The Celestine Forge is lit by molten brass and time-scripts below. Light sources brought by the party behave normally but occasionally flicker or change color without mechanical effect.

## Crawl Strategy Notes

**Marching Order.** Corridors between chambers are unstable — some stretch, some compress, some loop. Single-file is safest; double-file risks one column walking through a wall that phases out. The Inverted Archive's catwalk is 5 feet wide. The Prison of Forgotten Sentences has a spiral ramp (10 feet wide) and open central floor (100 feet diameter). The Celestine Forge's radial walkways are 5 feet wide and broken at the ends.

**Visibility.** Darkvision is necessary but not sufficient. The reality distortion means Darkvision occasionally shows things that aren't there (or fails to show things that are). At GM discretion, a PC relying solely on Darkvision must make a Will save (DC 15) once per stretch or perceive a minor hallucination — a door that isn't there, a shadow that moves wrong. This has no mechanical effect but builds tension. True Seeing negates this entirely and functions normally on this floor.

**Spot/Listen.** Key thresholds:
- Spot +15 or better: notices the fractured console's three glowing echoes in the Threshold before approaching (grants 1 round of observation before the puzzle begins)
- Listen +12 or better: hears the Marut's internal mechanisms in the Ember Sarcophagus from one chamber away
- Spot +18 or better: notices the dream-gem shards orbiting the sarcophagus are converging before the construct activates (grants a surprise round)

## Stretch Pacing

Floor 3 is the climax. It is shorter than Floor 2 in room count but denser in consequence. There are no safe rest points — the party should arrive rested or accept the risk. Corridors between chambers cost 1 stretch but may loop or double back (Will DC 15 to navigate correctly; failure costs an extra stretch).

### Room-by-Room Estimates

| Chamber | Estimated Stretches | Key Timing |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Corridors (between chambers) | 1–2 each | Will DC 15 to navigate; failure adds 1 stretch. |
| 1. Threshold of the Threadbare (EL 5) | 2–4 | Observing the three echoes takes 1 stretch each (3 total if methodical). Affirming them in order takes moments. A wrong order triggers combat (EL 10–11) and seals the correct door for 1 hour (6 stretches). |
| 2. The Inverted Archive (EL 2) | 1–3 | Each PC interacts with one memory (quick). Studying lore hooks takes longer — Comprehend Languages or Decipher Script to read scrolls. Low combat risk but high narrative density. |
| 3. The Prison of Forgotten Sentences (EL 6–15) | 3–6 | The lever puzzle is complex. Each pull + button press is quick, but the consequences (combat, traps, treasure) extend the time. If the party pulls all 15 non-exit levers, they face up to 7 combat encounters spread across 3–5 stretches. Cautious parties (pulling fewer levers) finish faster. |
| 4. The Chamber of Sundered Designs (EL 4) | 2–4 | One question per PC. Each question and answer takes a few minutes of roleplay. Feedback from improper use can extend this. The room rewards patience — rushing triggers backlash. |
| 5. The Ember Sarcophagus (EL 15) | 2–3 | The Marut activates when approached — combat interrupts stretch tracking. Post-combat, recovering the dream-gem and examining the chamber takes 1–2 stretches. |
| 6. The Celestine Forge (EL 18) | 1–∞ | The final encounter. If combat, resolve in initiative. If negotiation, this can span multiple stretches of roleplay. The ending is the ending — stretch tracking ceases here. |

**Total estimated stretches for Floor 3:** 15–30 (2.5–5 hours of in-game time)

### Relevant Activities (3.5e)

- **Search Area.** The Prison of Forgotten Sentences rewards careful searching (treasure hordes behind cell doors). The Threshold's console compartment requires affirming the echoes correctly, not a Search check.
- **Keep Watch.** There are no safe rest points. If the party must rest on this floor, every PC not resting should Keep Watch. Wandering encounters here are severe.
- **Interacting.** The Inverted Archive, Chamber of Sundered Designs, and Celestine Forge are all interaction-heavy. Reading fate-scripts, questioning the engine, and negotiating with Sirr al-Nar are the core activities.
- **Casting a Spell.** True Seeing is enormously valuable on this floor — it negates hallucinations, reveals the Threshold's correct door, and lets PCs read fate-scripts clearly. Tongues or Comprehend Languages for Celestine script. Freedom of Movement for the Prison's pit traps.
- **Take Your Time.** Taking 20 on Knowledge checks to study the fate-engine in the Chamber of Sundered Designs costs 2 stretches but may yield better questions. Whether this is worth the wandering encounter risk is a meaningful choice.

## Wandering Monster Table

Floor 3 has no safe zones. Wandering encounters represent the wound in reality bleeding hostile manifestations — these are not patrols but emergent threats.

**Encounter Frequency.** Constant — roll 1d6 at the end of every stretch.
**Encounter Likelihood.** Unlikely — on a 6 only, roll on the table.
**Encounter Difficulty.** Deadly — this floor does not forgive lingering. Every wandering encounter is a serious drain on resources the party needs for the Marut and Sirr al-Nar.
