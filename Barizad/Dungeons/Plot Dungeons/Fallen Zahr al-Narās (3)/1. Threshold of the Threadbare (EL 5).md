## Description

> The world folds in on itself and spits you out here — a hollow chamber where brass tiles peel like dried leaves and time itself limps along a broken leg.
> 
> There are three doorways, but none of them sit flush with the wall. One flickers forward and back a few feet like a looped breath. Another is frozen in mid-collapse, halfway through becoming rubble. The third appears sealed by a shifting spiral of script — words unwriting and rewriting themselves too fast to read.
> 
> In the center of the chamber spins a fractured console of brass rings. Three glowing echoes float silently around it, each flickering with the weight of memory. The exits pulse with half-sung prophecy — the doors themselves seem unsure whether they ever opened.
> 
> Around the ringed console are three words inlaid in silver:
> 
> “Act. Fracture. Conceal.”

## Mechanics
A successful Knowledge (history, arcana, or religion) DC 20 reveals that these are common verbs from old Celestine fate-scripts, used to denote pivotal branches in timeline evaluation.

### The Three Echoes
Each appears as a faint scene. PCs can observe and affirm one per round. Once all three are affirmed, the timeline stabilizes (or doesn’t, if wrong).

### The Child Conceals a Coin
- A Flamebound child sneaks into the chamber and hides a glowing coin behind a panel
- Caption appears in silver above the echo: “Concealment ensured continuity”

### The Warrior Shatters the Console
- A figure in red-hot armor slams a sword into the console, shattering it
- Caption: “Fracture broke the chain”

### The Adventurer Opens the Door
- A future-PC walks calmly to the spiral-sealed door and it opens
- Caption: “Action enables escape”

### The Actual Puzzle
The order the PCs affirm these echoes determines which future locks into place

If they affirm in the correct order — Conceal → Fracture → Act — the true door stabilizes and opens

The logic: The child hides the coin → The console is broken (ending the city's control) → A free agent (the adventurer) escapes.

### If They Choose the Wrong Order:
The incorrect door opens, revealing a time-warped trap or minor combat (e.g., shadows of themselves, EL 10–11)

The correct door seals for 1 hour unless dispelled, or unless someone sacrifices the dream-scripted coin

### If Solved Correctly:
The true door opens with a pulse of pressure — time realigns

PCs who contributed to the solution gain +1 insight bonus to Will saves for 24 hours while on this floor

A compartment in the center console reveals the Dream-Scripted Coin (usable later)
