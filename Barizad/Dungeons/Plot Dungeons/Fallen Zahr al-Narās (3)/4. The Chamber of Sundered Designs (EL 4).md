This is not just a lore room — it’s a roleplay-forward encounter and metaphysical puzzle. The party comes face to face with a shattered fragment of the city's original design engine — the device that once wrote destiny as formula, script, and shape.

The room allows the players to ask questions of fate itself, but at cost. It may answer prophecy, grant insight into possible futures, or even allow the party to redefine one aspect of their story — but it reacts unpredictably to paradox, defiance, or moral contradiction.

## Description

> A long, vaulted corridor opens into a hemispherical chamber of dark glass and metal veins. Suspended in the center of the room is a 20-foot-tall inverted tetrahedron of fractured brass and obsidian — a half-functioning fate-engine slowly spinning, occasionally halting and reversing.
> 
> Rings orbit its edges like erratic moons. Beneath it: a circular platform inscribed with {party size} glyphs — each glowing faintly when stepped upon. Above you, the vast geometrical form floats — cracked along one edge, spinning in stutters. Threads of brass connect it to the walls like snapped harp strings.
> 
> The air in this chamber is thin and tense, like a held breath. Words drift in the air: not sounds, but concepts, trying to be spoken. You feel something pressing against your will and you strain your mind to keep your memories and sense of self intact as the engine pushes-up against your past and future.

## Mechanics
The engine is still partially functional. The players may step on the glyph-platform and speak a question aloud — the Engine will respond, sometimes truthfully, sometimes dangerously.

Each PC may ask one question, once. A second attempt results in feedback (see below).

Each time the PCs ask a question one of the glyphs stops glowing.

### Types of Questions and Outcomes

| Type of Question                          | Engine Response                              |
| ----------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| Factual (e.g., “Where is the next gem?”)  | Truthful but symbolically phrased            |
| Philosophical (“Can fate be broken?”)     | Presents a riddle or paradox in response     |
| Personal (“Will I survive this journey?”) | Gives a vision of one possible ending        |
| Trick/Paradox (“What don’t you know?”)    | Engine stutters; backlash or partial insight |

Each answer can:
- Grant a clue or advantage for a future encounter
- Bestow a one-time reroll, insight bonus, or even alter the world slightly (e.g., “the door we need was never sealed”)

### Risks of Improper Use
Feedback is triggered If a PC:

- Lies while questioning
- Asks a self-contradictory question
- Asks a question after all glyphs have been extinguished

**Feeback (Su)**: Then the engine fractures further and deals one of the following (1d3):
- 1d4 Wisdom damage, no save (lasts 1 day)
- Reality Bleed: For 1 hour, their shadow moves independently
- Echo Affliction: Their speech becomes out of sync for 1 day, causing 20% spell failure chance for verbal components unless cured (break enchantment or greater restoration)

## Optional Interactions
- Comprehend Languages or Speak Language (Celestine) allows partial reading of the machine’s outer casing — including a partially erased name: Z—N—TH
