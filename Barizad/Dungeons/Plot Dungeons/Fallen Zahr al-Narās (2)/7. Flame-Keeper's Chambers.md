This is the bed chambers for the slaves the Efreet used to tend the Flamevault when the city was in-use.

## Description
> The air grows still as you cross the threshold into a vast square chamber of cracked obsidian tile and heat-dulled brass. Faint reddish light pulses along the floor like embers beneath skin, casting long shadows between low metal bunks bolted to the walls in rigid rows. Each has a shallow depression where a body once lay — not in comfort, but in compliance.
> 
> Hooks and brackets line the walls beside each bunk: restraints, now rusted through, and trays shaped like open hands. Above each station, the ceiling is etched with curling brass script in Ifriti glyphs — repeating the same command over and over:
> 
> "Endure. Obey. Feed the Flame."
> 
> A sense of deep exhaustion lingers in the stone. No threat stirs here.

This room is empty aside from the old bunks. There is no threat and no treasure. However, if the PCs rest here and seal the door they can do so safely (no encounters)

## The Vault
Across the hallway, there is a vault concealed behind false wall panel. The only indication that this vault exists is a glyph on the wall.

The vault is a 3 ft. deep recess behind a false wall panel (Search DC 22) secured with brass puzzle-lock runes — the kind typically used for service access, not treasury defense. A PC who has spent time in the dormitory and examined the wall glyphs gains a +2 bonus to identify the mechanism.

A successful Knowledge (the planes), Knowledge (arcana), or Decipher Script check (all DC 20) can determine the proper password from the glyphs
