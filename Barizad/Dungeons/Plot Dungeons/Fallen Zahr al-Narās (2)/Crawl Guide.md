# Fallen Zahr al-Narās (Floor 2) — Crawl Guide
*Levels 12–14 | EL 3–14 | Party of Four*

## General Features

**Construction Material.** Divine architecture — polished brass, black obsidian, and crystalline fixtures. This was the civic and ceremonial heart of Zahr al-Narās: auditoriums, vaults, dormitories, and fate-monitoring stations. The construction is grander than Floor 1 — wide corridors, vaulted ceilings, decorative inlays of gold and jet. Reality is less stable here: walls occasionally shimmer, distances feel inconsistent, and some corridors seem longer when walked than when observed.

**Ceiling Height.** Corridors are 15 feet high with vaulted arches. Chambers vary dramatically: the Brassfall Expanse is an open void with floating debris, the auditorium (Deus Extra Ludos) has tiered seating rising 30+ feet, and the Chamber of the Broken Line is an octagonal vault with 20-foot ceilings. The Firevault and Turntable have standard 15-foot ceilings.

**Lighting.** Dim supernatural glow throughout. The brass itself emits faint reddish-gold light — enough to see shapes at 30 feet but not enough to read or fight without penalty. The Brassfall Expanse has gravity-warped light from below. Deus Extra Ludos has crystalline chandeliers (many shattered, some still glowing dimly). The Flame-Keeper's Chambers pulse with ember-light from beneath the floor. Treat the default as dim light (20% miss chance, -2 to Spot checks) unless the party brings their own sources.

## Crawl Strategy Notes

**Marching Order.** Corridors are 15 feet wide — comfortable double-file with room for a third column. The Brassfall Expanse has no corridors — it's open traversal across floating debris (single-file on debris chunks). The Firevault and Turntable involve rotating floors that disrupt formation entirely. Plan for formations to break down in at least half the chambers.

**Visibility.** The dim supernatural glow means Darkvision is useful but not sufficient for full clarity beyond 60 feet. The Brassfall Expanse's gravity fluctuations create visual distortion — Spot checks take a -4 penalty beyond 30 feet. The Inverted Archive has inverted firelight that can disorient — first-time visitors must make a Will save (DC 12) or be shaken for 1 round.

**Spot/Listen.** Key thresholds for this floor:
- Spot +10 or better: notices the crystal orb slots in the Ecliptic Gate archway without a dedicated Search (DC 14)
- Listen +8 or better: hears the Clockwork Golems activating in the Firevault before entering
- Spot +12 or better: notices the false wall panel concealing the vault near the Flame-Keeper's Chambers (Search DC 22 — high Spot doesn't replace Search here, but it earns a hint)

## Stretch Pacing

Floor 2 is larger and more varied than Floor 1. Corridors between chambers cost 1–2 stretches depending on distance and the reality distortion. The floor has two safe rest points: rooms 5 (Deus Extra Ludos / the auditorium) and 7 (Flame-Keeper's Chambers). Resting anywhere else triggers a random encounter roll.

### Room-by-Room Estimates

| Chamber | Estimated Stretches | Key Timing |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Corridors (between chambers) | 1–2 each | Reality distortion makes some corridors longer than they appear. |
| 1. The Ecliptic Gate (EL 3) | 1–3 | A pure puzzle. Fast parties solve it in 1 stretch; trial-and-error with the 11 orbs can take 3. |
| 2. The Brassfall Expanse (EL 12) | 2–4 | Traversal + combat. Jumping between debris takes 1–2 stretches. The Gravity Elementals attack during traversal — combat interrupts stretch tracking. |
| 3. The Firevault (EL 14) | 3–5 | Rotating floors + locked doors + 6 Clockwork Golems. The gear-stopping mechanic is round-by-round but the overall challenge (clearing the room, picking locks, finding the spine rod) spans multiple stretches. |
| 4. The Turntable (EL 10) | 2–4 | Same rotating-floor mechanic as the Firevault. Gear manipulation and lock-picking under duress. |
| 5. Deus Extra Ludos (EL 9) | 2–6 | The play runs for several stretches if the party watches the full performance. If they engage with the riddle, add 1–2 more. This room is safe — no time pressure. |
| 6. Fate's Backstage | 1–2 | Treasure room. Search DC 20 takes 1 stretch; Taking 20 takes 2 stretches. |
| 7. Flame-Keeper's Chambers | 1–3 | Safe rest point. Exploring the dormitory takes 1 stretch. Finding and opening the vault (Search DC 22 + Knowledge DC 20) takes 1–2 more. |
| 8. The Heat-Leech Swarm (EL 7) | 1–2 | Combat encounter — resolve in initiative, then return to stretches. |
| 9. The Memory Kiln | 1–2 | Memory sacrifice mechanic — each PC interaction takes a few minutes, not a full stretch. |
| 10. The Chamber of the Broken Line (EL 13) | 2–4 | Boss fight + puzzle. Observing the three timeline echoes takes 1–2 stretches; combat with the Custodian interrupts tracking. The elevator descent afterward is automatic. |

**Total estimated stretches for Floor 2:** 20–40 (3.5–7 hours of in-game time)

### Relevant Activities (3.5e)

- **Search Area.** Critical for Fate's Backstage (DC 20), the Flame-Keeper's vault (DC 22), and debris in the Brassfall Expanse. Taking 20 where possible saves failed attempts but costs 2 stretches.
- **Keep Watch.** Important during rest at rooms 5 and 7. Also valuable while other PCs solve the Ecliptic Gate puzzle — a watcher can cover the party while they experiment.
- **Interacting.** Reading the Celestine script on walls, examining the automata in Deus Extra Ludos, studying the fate-engine fragments. Many chambers reward investigation with lore.
- **Casting a Spell.** Comprehend Languages or Tongues to read Celestine script. Detect Magic to examine the Ecliptic Gate orbs, the auditorium automata, or the spine rod in the Firevault. Identify on found items.
- **Helping Out.** Aid Another on Knowledge checks (history, arcana, the planes) to decipher lore — this floor rewards shared expertise.

**Encounter Likelihood.** Possible — on a 5 or 6, roll on the table.
**Encounter Difficulty.** Challenging to Deadly — this floor is deep in hostile territory. Wandering monsters are serious threats, not speed bumps.
