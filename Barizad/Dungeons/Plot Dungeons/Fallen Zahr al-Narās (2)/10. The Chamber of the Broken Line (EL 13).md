> The chamber ahead is an eight-sided vault of brass and shadow, lit by no flame, yet softly aglow from beneath the floor. Its tiles are polished obsidian veined with gold — a network of broken lines converging at a central disc of fused glass, cracked and ringed by half-erased sigils.
> 
> The walls pulse with gentle breath-like pressure, the way lungs might exhale between words. Great circular devices — something between astrolabe and throne — are recessed into each wall segment, once likely used to monitor fate threads or control magical trajectories.
> 
> The air is still, but it is not silent: you hear whispers not with your ears, but in your pulse — fragments of voices that never were, now leaking through timelines like a hairline fracture in reality.

### Encounter Activation
When the PCs approach the disc:

> As you step onto the central platform, the air shifts. One of the eight wall-instruments hums to life. Sparks drift from it like fireflies. Then, with a flicker, a figure coalesces in the air above the disc — floating, statuesque, and incomplete.
> 
> Its limbs are brass and glass, its wings an impossible geometry of cut crystal spinning like gears. A scroll of flaming script unfurls from behind its head and circles the room like a halo. But even from here, you can see: the script is stuttering — lines written and overwritten in contradiction.
> 
> Its voice is twofold: one tone commanding, one regretful.
> 
> “Deviation from Prescribed Design: Detected. Correction Protocol: Active. Please submit to reformatting.”
> 
> The battle with the Custodian of the Broken Line begins.

### Treasure
> After the Custodian is defeated, the central platform descends 2 feet with a clunk. One of the eight wall-thrones unfolds like a blooming astrolabe, revealing a compartment filled with interlocking brass puzzle-plates and a crystalline matrix humming with residual chronomantic energy.

Inside are:

Ring of Evasion (DMG, 45,000 gp)
- A thin golden ring with runes shaped like coiling flame and a leaping figure. It grants the wearer the ability to avoid magical explosions and unstable space with uncanny grace.
- Effect: As per standard item — if the wearer makes a successful Reflex saving throw against an attack that normally deals half damage on a save, they instead take no damage.  

Pearl of Power (4th level) (DMG, 16,000 gp)
- Found suspended in a glassy tear-shaped container marked with unknown dates.
- Effect: Once per day, allows the retrieval of a previously cast 4th-level spell.  

Incense of Meditation (1 dose) (DMG, 4,900 gp)
- Wrapped in a scroll of geometric hymns. The incense smells faintly of brass and dried fig.
- Effect: If burned during preparation, it boosts divine caster's effective level for spell DCs and caster level checks for 24 hours (standard effect).   

Coins and Art Objects
- 1,300 gp in ancient, square-cut coins bearing the seal of the Celestine Furnace
- A sundial-sized astrolabe (worth 1,000 gp) made of electrum and black jade, showing long-expired celestial alignments
- A silver and obsidian meditation dish inlaid with Fate-script (worth 800 gp) 

### Descent
A few minutes later the PCs start hearing a whirring noise like some mechanism is coming to life. The platform is, in fact, an elevator and will start its descent. Very slowly at first but gaining speed quickly after. The PCs can take it to the deepest level of the dungeon. If they choose to go-back and rest first the lift returns every 20m.
