### Description

> The hidden panel sighs open behind the stage, revealing a narrow stair that descends into silence. The walls here are close and warm, like a forgotten breath still clinging to the stone. Brass piping curves along the ceiling, many of them cracked and weeping tiny flickers of illusory light — remnants of old magic bleeding out of the infrastructure.
> 
> The stair ends in a shallow chamber carved not for grandeur, but for control. It is a director’s sanctum, or perhaps a shrine to authorship. Racks of burnt scripts and scroll-tubes line the walls, many warped or melted, their glyphs sloughing like ash. A collapsed golem, shaped like a performer mid-bow, lies rusted near the far corner. Its face bears no features — only an uncut mirror of polished brass.
> 
> At the center stands a small dais with a broken mask laid atop it. Behind it, half-retracted into the wall, rests a brass weapon prop — stylized, ornate, but humming with real power.
> 
> There is no dust on the pedestal.
> 
> Someone placed these here for you.

Searching the room (Search DC 20) reveals the following treasures:

### Treasure

**Rod of Cancellation** (DMG, 11,000gp)
- Symbolic of severing fate or “cancelling” magic from the original script
- Found in a hollow prop sword, labeled "Final Line (unused)"  

**Scroll of Greater Dispel Magic** & **Scroll of Ture Seeing**
- Found inside a sealed director’s tube etched with Celestine script  

**Circlet of Persuasion** (DMG, 4,500gp)
- Styled as a bronze theatrical diadem with half-masks on either side
- Boosts Charisma-based checks — appropriate for someone who “spoke fate  

**Fragment of the Redacted Design**
- A burnt page of the master script that stops mid-sentence and then resumes in a different handwriting
- Studying it (Arcana or Decipher Script DC 25) reveals:
	- The existence of multiple “drafts” of fate
	- That Sirr al-Nar edited his own fate (and possibly the players') 

**Half-Burned Mask of the Arbiter**
- Cracked porcelain with brass teeth, stylized after Sirr al-Nar’s regalia
- When held, it whispers his old name and titles in reverse
- Grants no mechanical effect, but may affect roleplay or interactions later
