_Adapted from: The Slag Pits (Broadsheet 4.02)_

1. **Description**

    1. A series of narrow brass catwalks bridge a vast pit of molten slag — waste heat from the Celestine Furnace, still churning centuries after the Fall
    2. At the chamber's center, suspended by massive chains, hangs a brass sphere 10 feet in diameter: a heat-exchange node that once regulated temperature across an entire city district
    3. Two side chambers (north and south) house ventricle mechanisms that funnel excess heat away. Both must be closed to force enough thermal energy into the node to reactivate it
    4. The heat is immediate and oppressive. The brass catwalks are warm to the touch. The slag below glows white-orange.

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **Scorching Heat**: At the end of every 10 minutes a creature spends in this chamber, that creature must make a Fortitude save (DC 16) or take 2d6 points of nonlethal damage and become fatigued. Creatures already fatigued become exhausted instead. Creatures with fire resistance reduce the DC by their resistance value. Creatures with fire immunity automatically succeed.
    2. **Escalation**: Each time the party closes a ventricle, the heat intensifies:
        1. _One ventricle closed_: the Fortitude save must be made every minute (10 rounds) instead of every 10 minutes, and the DC increases to 18
        2. _Both ventricles closed_: the save must be made every round at DC 20. The node reactivates in 5 rounds, at which point the heat stabilizes and the exit unseals.
    3. **The Catwalks**: The catwalks are 5 feet wide with no railings. A creature that is bull rushed, knocked prone by damage, or fails a Balance check while moving at full speed (DC 14) risks falling. A creature that goes over the edge may attempt a Reflex save (DC 18) to grab the edge and hang on.
    4. **Molten Slag**: Any creature that falls in takes 15d6 fire damage per round. Objects take the same damage.
    5. **North Ventricle**: In good condition. A creature adjacent to the valve can close it as a full-round action with a Strength check (DC 16).
    6. **South Ventricle**: Badly damaged. A successful Craft (blacksmithing) or Knowledge (architecture/engineering) check (DC 20) followed by 10 minutes of repair work restores it to working order. It can then be closed as a full-round action with a Strength check (DC 16).
    7. **Protective Gear**: A locked storage alcove (Open Lock DC 24, or break: Hardness 10, 40 hp) near the chamber entrance contains two sets of heavy brass-plated coveralls. While wearing a set, a creature's speed is halved but it gains fire resistance 15.

3. **Encounters**

    1. The north ventricle chamber contains a **Noble Salamander** (MM 219) who has claimed the heat vent as a personal domain. It commands the space with authority and attacks anything that approaches the valve. It fights with its +3 longspear and uses Wall of Fire to cut off retreat along the catwalks.
    2. A **Huge Fire Elemental** (MM 99) patrols the catwalks, emerging from the slag pit every 1d4 minutes. It attacks non-fire creatures on sight but defers to the Noble Salamander.

4. **Treasure**

    1. In the storage alcove alongside the protective gear: a _+2 Heavy Steel Shield_ with fire resistance 10 (as _Shield of Fire Resistance_, worth ~8,000 gp)
    2. The Noble Salamander's lair contains: a cracked brass orrery worth 1,500 gp to a collector, a potion of _Protection from Energy (Fire)_, a _+2 Longspear_ with Ignan runes (the tip burns with a steady flame), and a _Ring of Minor Energy Resistance (Fire)_ (DMG, fire resistance 10, worth 12,000 gp)
    3. If the node is reactivated and examined (Search DC 20): a fist-sized True Brass ingot (component for crafting fire-resistant equipment, worth 2,500 gp) and a _Phylactery of Faithfulness_ (DMG, worth 1,000 gp) fused to the node's inner casing
