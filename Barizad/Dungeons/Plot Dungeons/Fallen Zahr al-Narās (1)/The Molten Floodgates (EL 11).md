_Adapted from: The Floodgates (Broadsheet 4.02)_

1. **Description**

    1. A cistern complex that once controlled the flow of molten brass through the city's industrial district — three massive valves (A, B, C) regulate channels that feed into a central reservoir
    2. When the party arrives, two **Fire Giants** and their squad of Azer are in the process of opening the valves to divert molten brass into the corridors the party has already traversed — cutting off their route back
    3. Valve A is already open. Valves B and C are still closed. One giant is working on Valve B while the other stands guard.

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **The Objective**: Prevent the fire giants from opening all three valves. If all three open, the corridors behind the party flood with molten brass in 1d4+1 rounds, forcing them to find an alternate (and more dangerous) route forward.
    2. **Operating a Valve**: A creature adjacent to a valve can open or close it as a full-round action. A Large or larger creature does this automatically. A Medium or smaller creature must succeed on a Strength check (DC 22) to turn the valve. _Aid Another works normally._
    3. **Molten Brass Channels**: Three channels of flowing molten brass bisect the room, each 10 feet wide. Any creature that falls in or starts its turn in molten brass takes 15d6 fire damage. The channels can be crossed by:
        1. Jumping (Jump DC 10 with a running start, DC 20 from standing)
        2. Using the narrow maintenance walkways that bridge each channel (5 feet wide, Balance DC 8 under normal conditions, DC 15 during combat or if the walkway is damaged)
    4. **The Fire Giants' Strategy**: They work in tandem — one opens valves while the other covers her with rock throwing and melee. They use their Azer to block chokepoints on the maintenance walkways. They are willing to talk but consider this their sworn duty and will not be persuaded to stop (Diplomacy is ineffective; Intimidate DC 34 to make one hesitate for 1 round).
    5. **Closing Valve A**: The party can also go on offense by closing already-open valves. Valve A requires the same Strength check and has 2 Azer guarding it.
    6. **Victory Condition**: If the party closes all three valves, the cistern's safety mechanism locks the valves in place and a sealed exit on the far side grinds open. If the party merely prevents the giants from opening all three (e.g., by killing or driving them off), the same exit opens but the route back also remains passable.

3. **Encounters**

    1. 2x **Fire Giants** (MM 121, CR 10 each) — one fights defensively while prioritizing the valves; the other engages the party directly, using rock throwing to keep PCs at range and Cleave/Great Cleave against clusters on the walkways.
    2. 6x **Azer** (MM 21, CR 2) — they serve as blockers and skirmishers, positioning themselves on the maintenance walkways and at valve stations. Their fire aura makes grappling or Bull Rushing them off the walkways risky.
    3. The fire giants will attempt to retreat (taking surviving Azer with them) if both are reduced below 60 hp. They may reappear later in the dungeon at the DM's discretion.

4. **Treasure**

    1. The fire giants carry between them: a _Bag of Holding_ (Type II) containing 4,800 gp, a bronze statuette of a Dahrin prince (750 gp), 4 potions of _Cure Serious Wounds_, and a _Potion of Haste_
    2. Each Azer carries a Masterwork warhammer and 30 gp in brass coinage
    3. In a maintenance closet near Valve C (Search DC 18): a set of Masterwork Thieves' Tools wrapped in fireproof cloth, a _Wand of Shatter_ (CL 5, 22 charges), and a _Vest of Resistance +2_ (4,000 gp) sized for a Medium creature — likely belonged to a Kibrin maintenance worker
