_Adapted from: The Cold Forge (Broadsheet 4.02)_

1. **Description**

    1. This long, vaulted chamber once processed fuel for the Celestine Furnace — natural gas drawn from deep beneath the city, refined and piped upward
    2. The forge at the chamber's center is cold and dark. The air is thick with a sweet, foul-smelling haze
    3. A broken brass pipe 30 feet overhead pours gas into the room from a cracked junction. The platform beside it is accessible by climbing the wall
    4. A bellows sub-chamber sits beneath the main forge floor, accessed by a narrow staircase

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **Toxic Gas**: While the chamber is filled with gas, any creature that spends 10 minutes in the room must succeed on a Fortitude save (DC 18) or become nauseated for 1 hour. A creature that fails by 5 or more falls unconscious for as long as it is nauseated (or until it takes damage). If the pipe is repaired, the gas dissipates in 10 minutes.
    2. **Combustible**: If the gas contacts an open flame (torch, fire spell, flaming weapon), it detonates. All creatures in the chamber take 12d6 fire damage (Reflex DC 18 for half). While the pipe remains broken, the chamber refills with gas in 10 minutes. _This should be telegraphed clearly — the gas smells sharp and chemical, and a Knowledge (architecture/engineering) or Craft (alchemy) check (DC 12) identifies the danger immediately._
    3. **Repairing the Pipe**:
        1. Climbing the wall to the junction platform requires a Climb check (DC 18). The platform is 30 feet up.
        2. A creature within 5 feet of the pipe can force the broken sections together with a Strength check (DC 24) as a full-round action. The creature must use both hands to hold the pipe in place.
        3. While held, another creature must spend 10 minutes securing the pipe with rope, chain, or Use Rope (DC 15). This permanently repairs the pipe and allows gas to flow correctly to the forge.
    4. **Lighting the Forge**:
        1. Three conditions must be met simultaneously: gas must be flowing (pipe repaired), air must be supplied (bellows worked), and flame must be applied to the forge heart
        2. Working the bellows requires a creature in the sub-chamber to spend a standard action each round pumping. Air is supplied only until the start of that creature's next turn.
        3. Applying flame to the forge while gas flows and bellows are pumped ignites it. The forge roars to life and a sealed brass door at the far end of the chamber grinds open.
    5. **The core puzzle**: The obvious solution (light a fire) will kill everyone. The party must fix the pipe first, which requires one person climbing and holding the pipe with both hands while another secures it — all while the gas is making people sick. Then they need at least two people coordinating (bellows + ignition) to light the forge safely. Minimum three-person coordination.

3. **Encounters**

    1. The bellows sub-chamber contains a **Huge Fire Elemental** (MM 99) that has claimed the dormant bellows as a lair. It attacks when disturbed but is not hostile to creatures with fire subtype. It can be bargained with (Diplomacy DC 24, or offer it a substantial source of fuel/flame) to work the bellows instead.
    2. The junction platform is occupied by 2 **Salamanders** (MM 219) who have made a forge-nest in the heat vent. They attack anything that approaches and fight tactically — one uses its tail to grapple climbers while the other strikes with its longspear. _If the gas has not been cleared, their fire attacks risk detonating the room._

4. **Treasure**

    1. In the bellows sub-chamber: a set of Masterwork Artisan's Tools (glassblowing) etched with Ignan script (75 gp) and a _Cloak of Resistance +1_ (1,000 gp) draped over a forgotten workbench
    2. On the junction platform: a brass-and-ruby valve handle that functions as an _Eversmoking Bottle_ (DMG 254)
    3. In a sealed cabinet near the forge (Search DC 20): 2 potions of _Protection from Energy (Fire)_ and a scroll of _Wind Wall_ (CL 5)
    4. Coins scattered across the forge floor: 800 gp, 120 pp
    5. The salamanders' nest contains a _+1 Flaming Longspear_ with Ignan runes and a fire opal worth 1,000 gp
