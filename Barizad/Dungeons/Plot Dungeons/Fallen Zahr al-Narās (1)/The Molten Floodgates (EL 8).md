_Adapted from: The Floodgates (Broadsheet 4.02)_

1. **Description**

    1. A cistern complex that once controlled the flow of molten brass through the city's industrial district — three massive valves (A, B, C) regulate channels that feed into a central reservoir
    2. When the party arrives, a **Fire Giant** and her squad of Azer are in the process of opening the valves to divert molten brass into the corridors the party has already traversed — cutting off their route back
    3. Valve A is already open. Valves B and C are still closed. The giant is working on Valve B when the party enters.

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **The Objective**: Prevent the fire giant from opening all three valves. If all three open, the corridors behind the party flood with molten brass in 1d4+1 rounds, forcing them to find an alternate (and more dangerous) route forward.
    2. **Operating a Valve**: A creature adjacent to a valve can open or close it as a full-round action. A Large or larger creature does this automatically. A Medium or smaller creature must succeed on a Strength check (DC 18) to turn the valve. _Aid Another works normally._
    3. **Molten Brass Channels**: Three channels of flowing molten brass bisect the room, each 10 feet wide. Any creature that falls in or starts its turn in molten brass takes 10d6 fire damage. The channels can be crossed by:
        1. Jumping (Jump DC 10 with a running start, DC 20 from standing)
        2. Using the narrow maintenance walkways that bridge each channel (5 feet wide, Balance DC 5 under normal conditions, DC 12 during combat or if the walkway is damaged)
    4. **The Fire Giant's Strategy**: She opens valves in order (B, then C). She uses her Azer to block the party from reaching the valves. She is willing to talk but considers this her duty and will not be persuaded to stop (Diplomacy is ineffective; Intimidate DC 30 to make her hesitate for 1 round).
    5. **Closing Valve A**: The party can also go on offense by closing already-open valves. Valve A requires the same Strength check and has 2 Azer guarding it.
    6. **Victory Condition**: If the party closes all three valves, the cistern's safety mechanism locks the valves in place and a sealed exit on the far side grinds open. If the party merely prevents the giant from opening all three (e.g., by killing or driving her off), the same exit opens but the route back also remains passable.

3. **Encounters**

    1. 1x **Fire Giant** (MM 121, CR 10) — she fights defensively, prioritizing opening valves over engaging the party. She uses rock throwing to keep PCs at range while she works.
    2. 4x **Azer** (MM 21, CR 2) — they serve as blockers and skirmishers, positioning themselves on the maintenance walkways and at valve stations. Their fire aura makes grappling or Bull Rushing them off the walkways risky.
    3. The fire giant will attempt to retreat (taking 1d4 surviving Azer with her) if reduced below 40 hp. She may reappear later in the dungeon at the DM's discretion.

4. **Treasure**

    1. The fire giant carries: a _Bag of Holding_ (Type I) containing 2,400 gp, a bronze statuette of a Dahrin prince (350 gp), and 3 potions of Cure Serious Wounds
    2. Each Azer carries a Masterwork warhammer and 20 gp in brass coinage
    3. In a maintenance closet near Valve C (Search DC 15): a set of Masterwork Thieves' Tools wrapped in fireproof cloth, and a _Wand of Shatter_ (CL 3, 18 charges)
