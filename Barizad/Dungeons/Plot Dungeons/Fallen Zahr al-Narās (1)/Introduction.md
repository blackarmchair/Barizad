## Purpose

- Establish the fallen city as a real, dangerous place before the party reaches the Ecliptic Gate
- Introduce the physics of a broken divine engine: residual heat, warped gravity, frozen time
- Teach dungeon-crawl survival skills — resource management, cooperative problem-solving, environmental reading
- Foreshadow the Celestine Furnace (heat vents, forge-works, prophecy infrastructure)

## Themes

- Industrial ruin — forge-works, gaslines, workshops, ventilation shafts
- A city that was built for efreeti, not mortals — the heat alone is hostile
- Broken systems still running: automated processes half-completing, flames venting on schedule, golems following obsolete orders
- Evidence of the Fall everywhere — frozen battles, collapsed infrastructure, time-locked wreckage

## Tone

This floor is physically punishing. The rooms demand cooperation, planning, and willingness to split the party's attention across simultaneous objectives. Combat is secondary to environment — the city itself is the enemy.

## Level Range

- EL 5–9
- Designed for a party of four at levels 5–7

## Structure

Five chambers connected by brass corridors. The party can encounter them in any order except the Furnace Shaft, which should come last and leads down toward the Ecliptic Gate on Floor 2.

1. The Shattered Gasworks (EL 7)
2. The Crucible Walk (EL 8)
3. The Frozen Processional (EL 9)
4. The Molten Floodgates (EL 8)
5. The Furnace Shaft (EL 7)
