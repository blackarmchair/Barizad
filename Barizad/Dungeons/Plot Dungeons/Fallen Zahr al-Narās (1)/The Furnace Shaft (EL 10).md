_Adapted from: The Yawning Abyss (Broadsheet 4.02)_

1. **Description**

    1. A vast vertical shaft descending into darkness — one of the primary ventilation and transport shafts for the Celestine Furnace, now broken and silent
    2. The shaft is 30 feet in diameter. Light sources dropped into it are swallowed by the dark; objects thrown down produce no audible impact. _This shaft connects Floor 1 to Floor 2 and ultimately to the Ecliptic Gate._
    3. A massive brass crane is bolted to the shaft wall near the top. Its arm extends over the void, suspending a cargo platform on frayed chains approximately 130 feet below the party's entry point.
    4. Side chambers open directly onto the shaft wall at various depths: crane controls at 100 feet down, a sealed workshop at 150 feet, and the exit passage at 200 feet

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **The Shaft**: Falling means death. The shaft extends far deeper than 200 feet — what lies at the true bottom is unknown (perhaps the Furnace core itself, perhaps something worse). Make this unambiguously clear to the players.
    2. **Climbing the Shaft**: The interior walls are rough brass with plenty of handholds. Climb DC 18 to descend safely. A failed check by 5 or more means a fall.
    3. **The Crane**:
        1. The cargo platform hangs 130 feet below the entry point. It can be raised or lowered from the crane control room.
        2. **Crane Controls**: Located in a side chamber at 100 feet depth. A successful Knowledge (architecture/engineering) check (DC 18) is required to operate the controls. A creature that fails can spend 10 minutes studying them to succeed automatically.
        3. **Weight Limit**: The chains are badly corroded. The platform can safely support 600 lbs. If the weight exceeds 600 lbs, one of the three chains snaps (the platform tilts — all creatures on it must make a Reflex save DC 17 or slide off). If the weight still exceeds 600 lbs at the end of the round, the platform collapses entirely.
        4. _A party of four in armor with gear will likely exceed 600 lbs. They must split into groups or find another way down._
    4. **The Side Chambers**:
        1. **Crane Controls (100 ft)**: See above. Also contains a Kibrin worker's journal (in Ignan) describing routine maintenance — a Knowledge (history) or Decipher Script check (DC 15) reveals it mentions a "treasure vault" deeper in the shaft and warns of "the guardian that does not sleep."
        2. **Sealed Workshop (150 ft)**: A heavy brass door (Open Lock DC 26, or Hardness 10, 80 hp) protects a Kibrin golem-maintenance workshop. Inside: golem-repair tools (function as Masterwork artisan's tools for Craft (golem) checks), spare parts, and a dormant **Brass Golem** (use Iron Golem, MM 136, with fire immunity instead of fire absorption; CR 13). The golem activates and attacks if any tools or parts are removed from the room. _Its patrol orders are centuries out of date — it fights to kill but does not pursue beyond the workshop door. A party that shuts the door behind them is safe._
        3. **Exit Passage (200 ft)**: A horizontal corridor leading away from the shaft — this connects to the approach to the Ecliptic Gate on Floor 2. No encounter here; the transition should feel like a breath of relief after the descent.
    5. **Alternative Descent**: Fly, Levitate, Feather Fall, Spider Climb, and similar spells all work normally in the shaft. Rope (200 ft needed to reach the exit) can be anchored at the top — the party likely has enough if they pool resources, but this requires trusting the anchor point (a Search check DC 15 identifies a solid brass ring meant for this purpose).

3. **Encounters**

    1. The sealed workshop's **Brass Golem** (see above, CR 13). It does not pursue beyond the workshop door. _At this level, the golem is a genuine threat — parties should be warned by the journal entry and the sheer weight of the brass door. Fighting it is optional but costly._
    2. A **Huge Air Elemental** (MM 96) inhabits the shaft, drawn by the constant updraft. It is territorial and aggressive — it attacks creatures using the crane or flying through its space, using Whirlwind to hurl them off the platform or away from the walls. It can be calmed with a successful Wild Empathy or Diplomacy check (DC 24). If calmed, it ignores the party. If befriended (DC 30), it can carry one Medium creature up or down the shaft safely.

4. **Treasure**

    1. In the crane control room: 150 gp in brass coins, a dented brass flask that functions as a _Decanter of Endless Water_ (DMG 254) — the command words are inscribed on the base in Ignan
    2. In the sealed workshop: golem-repair tools (150 gp as artisan's tools), a set of 6 True Brass bolts (function as _+2 Bolts_ against constructs only, bypassing hardness), a _Handy Haversack_ (DMG, 2,000 gp) used by the workshop's former overseer, and a brass gear-key that fits an unknown lock deeper in the ruins (plot item)
    3. If the air elemental is defeated: a _Bag of Wind_ (as _Bag of Tricks, Gray_, DMG 248, except it produces Medium Air Elementals instead of animals — 5 uses remaining)
