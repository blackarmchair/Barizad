_Adapted from: The War in Waiting (Broadsheet 4.02)_

1. **Description**

    1. A broad ceremonial avenue — once used for formal processions between the Dahrin courts and the Celestine Furnace — frozen in a single instant of catastrophe
    2. Two efreeti hover 30 feet above the street, mid-combat: one in ornate Dahrin court armor (a noble who tried to stop the Fall), the other in Jannat al-Ruh scholar's robes (a furnace-priest loyal to Sirr al-Nar). Fire, brass shrapnel, and spell effects hang motionless in the air between them.
    3. Along the avenue, side chambers (former shops and offices) contain additional frozen figures: Kibrin laborers mid-flee, golem-sentries mid-activation, a cluster of mephit servants caught in the blast wave
    4. Everything is silent. Dust motes hang motionless. The temperature is exactly as it was at the moment of the Fall.

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **Frozen Time**: All creatures and objects in this chamber are affected by a permanent _Temporal Stasis_-like effect. A Knowledge (arcana) check (DC 18) identifies the nature of the effect. A Spellcraft check (DC 24) determines that a powerful artifact must be sustaining it.
    2. **The Sustaining Object**: A brass hourglass the size of a human fist floats in one of the side chambers, frozen mid-tumble. It radiates overwhelming abjuration magic (CL 20+). _This is the source of the effect._ Moving or damaging it ends the stasis on everything simultaneously.
    3. **Triggering the Unfreeze**: The stasis ends immediately if:
        1. Any frozen creature or object takes damage
        2. Any frozen creature or object is physically moved (including taking items from their person)
        3. The hourglass is moved or damaged
    4. **The Key**: The Dahrin noble wears a brass signet ring on a chain around his neck. This ring is needed to operate a mechanism deeper in the ruins (DM's choice — a door, a lift, a ward). Removing it unfreezes everything.
    5. **When the Stasis Breaks**: Roll initiative immediately. All frozen creatures unfreeze and continue exactly what they were doing:
        1. The two efreeti resume their fight. The **Dahrin Noble** (Efreeti with 4 Fighter levels, CR 12, MM 115) wields a _+2 flaming scimitar_ and wears the court armor. The **Furnace-Priest** (Efreeti with 4 Cleric levels, CR 12, MM 115) casts _Flame Strike_ and _Unholy Blight_ in addition to standard efreeti abilities. Each considers the other a greater threat than the party, but they will attack PCs who interfere or get caught in the crossfire.
        2. Unexplored side chambers: roll 1d6 for each. On a 1–2, the chamber contains 1d4 Kibrin laborers (use Commoner stats) who flee in panic. On a 3–5, it contains 1d2 **Brass Golems** (use Iron Golem, MM 136, with fire immunity instead of fire absorption and Str 31 instead of 33) mid-activation who attack the nearest creatures. On a 6, it contains 1d4 **Salamanders** (MM 219) caught in a collapsing corridor — all creatures within 15 feet take 8d6 fire damage (Reflex DC 18 half) from the blast resuming.
    6. **Diplomacy**: If one efreeti is badly losing, it will attempt to parley with the party (Diplomacy DC 20 to shift to Indifferent). The Dahrin noble can share information about the city's layout and Sirr al-Nar's betrayal. The furnace-priest is fanatical and will not negotiate.
    7. **Avoiding the Fight**: A clever party might try to remove the ring without triggering the stasis break. This is extremely difficult: the ring must be slid off the chain without moving the efreeti's body. Sleight of Hand check (DC 28) with a -4 penalty for the efreeti's size. A failure by 5 or more jostles the body and triggers the unfreeze.

3. **Encounters**

    1. **Dahrin Noble** — Efreeti Fighter 4 (CR 12) — hostile to the furnace-priest, wary of the party
    2. **Furnace-Priest** — Efreeti Cleric 4 (CR 12) — fanatical, hostile to all non-loyalists
    3. Variable side-chamber encounters (see above) — likely includes 1–2 Brass Golems (CR 13 each)
    4. The encounter can be avoided entirely with a DC 28 Sleight of Hand check

4. **Treasure**

    1. The brass signet ring (plot item, also functions as a _Ring of Fire Resistance, Major_ — fire resistance 20)
    2. The Dahrin noble's court armor: a set of _+2 Mithral Breastplate_ sized for a Large creature (can be resized by an armorsmith, 1 week of work)
    3. The furnace-priest's staff: a _Staff of Fire_ (DMG 244) with 14 charges remaining
    4. The Dahrin noble's scimitar: a _+2 Flaming Scimitar_ sized for a Large creature (can be resized)
    5. The brass hourglass: if recovered intact (Disable Device DC 28 to remove it without triggering the effect), it functions as a one-use _Temporal Stasis_ effect (CL 17, Will DC 23 negates). If the stasis has already been broken, the hourglass is inert but worth 3,500 gp as an art object.
    6. Scattered across the avenue: 300 gp in assorted pre-Fall brass coins (historical curiosity, worth 3x face value to a collector)
