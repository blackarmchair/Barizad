1. **Description**

    1. A row of eight stone cells, each 10 feet square, carved into the volcanic rock and sealed with copper-banded doors fitted with viewing slits. The corridor between them is 5 feet wide and stifling — the cells radiate heat and something else, something that presses against the mind like a low-frequency hum
    2. Inside each occupied cell, an oracle lies on a stone slab in deep trance — dream-fasting, the Aram-Zaneth's method of inducing prophetic vision through sensory deprivation, dehydration, and sacred incense exposure. The oracles are emaciated, their bodies marked with burn scars and ash-paste sigils. Their eyes are closed but moving rapidly beneath the lids
    3. The cells glow faintly — each oracle's body produces a dim amber luminescence from the sigils on their skin. The glow pulses in sync with their breathing. The air near the cells smells of ozone and burnt copper
    4. One cell — the fifth from the entrance — has its door slightly ajar. The glow from inside is different: cooler, bluer, flickering rather than pulsing

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **Psychic Backlash**: Disturbing a dreaming oracle (opening a cell door, touching an oracle, making loud noise within 10 feet of an occupied cell) triggers psychic backlash in a 15-foot radius. All creatures in the area must make a Will save (DC 16) or take 2d6 points of Wisdom damage and be stunned for 1 round. Creatures that succeed take 1d4 Wisdom damage and are dazed for 1 round. This is a mind-affecting effect. Each oracle can only trigger one backlash — after that, the oracle wakes (disoriented, non-combatant, Commoner 1 with 3 hp)
    2. **Investigating Cells Safely**: The viewing slits allow observation without opening the door (no backlash). Spot DC 14 through the slit to assess the oracle's condition. A Heal check (DC 16) through the slit identifies which oracles are near death (two of them — cells 2 and 7). A Knowledge (arcana) or Spellcraft DC 14 check identifies the ash-paste sigils as a variant of Magic Circle that channels the oracle's visions into the Temple's prophetic infrastructure
    3. **Cell Occupancy**:
        1. _Cells 1, 3, 4, 6_: Occupied by dreaming oracles. Standard psychic backlash if disturbed. Nothing of note beyond the oracles themselves
        2. _Cell 2_: Occupied by a dying oracle. If disturbed, the backlash is weaker (Will DC 12, 1d4 Wisdom damage only). The oracle whispers a final prophecy before dying: "The one who drinks the brass sees all endings but chooses none" — a reference to the High Oracle
        3. _Cell 5_: The ajar door. Contains the captive (see below)
        4. _Cell 7_: Occupied by a dying oracle. If disturbed, the backlash is stronger than normal (Will DC 18, 3d6 Wisdom damage) — this oracle's visions are violent and fractured. She screams about "the ten verdicts" (the dream-gems) before collapsing
        5. _Cell 8_: Empty. Contains only a stone slab and a set of manacles. Search DC 16: scratched into the wall in Common — "They see nothing. They hear nothing. The fire speaks to no one."
    4. **Searching Without Triggering**: A party member can check each cell through the viewing slit (1 stretch per 2 cells) without triggering backlash. Search DC 16 (through the slit) to determine which cell holds a non-oracle occupant — the blue glow in Cell 5 is the tell

3. **Encounters**

    1. **Cell 5 — The Captive**: A woman named Sulha, an Aram-Zaneth priestess (Cleric 5 of Zaneth) who asked the wrong questions about the Archive of Heresies and was imprisoned for heresy. She has been in the cell for three days, subjected to dream-fasting against her will. She is lucid but weakened (currently at 8 hp, 4 Wisdom damage from the fasting process)
        1. _Information_: Sulha knows Floor 3's layout and can describe each room. She reveals: the High Oracle is in the Molten Font, the Reliquary is trapped and guarded by a construct, and the Pyre Ascendant is where Naehri holds court. She also knows that the High Oracle is not hostile by default — she is an ancient who has drunk molten brass so long that she exists in a state of perpetual vision. "She knows the truth. She has always known. She considers it irrelevant"
        2. _Condition_: Sulha will not fight for the party but will guide them through Floor 3 if freed. She requests only that the party bring the suppressed texts from the Archive to the Oracle — "Let her deny it to her face. Let her deny what's written in her own Temple's hand"
        3. _Trust_: Sulha is genuine. Sense Motive DC 12 confirms she is not lying. She is terrified of the Thirsting Light ("Naehri sees heresy everywhere — she would have burned the Archive if the Oracle permitted it")
    2. If the party triggers backlash from multiple cells simultaneously (opening two or more doors at once), the psychic feedback resonates. All creatures on Floor 2 must make a Will save (DC 14) or be dazed for 1 round. This alerts every creature on the floor to the party's location

4. **Treasure**

    1. Cell 5 (Sulha's cell): Sulha's confiscated equipment is in a locked chest by the door (Open Lock DC 16): a _+1 Light Mace_, a _Periapt of Wisdom +2_ (DMG pg. 263, worth 4,000 gp), a _Scroll of Restoration_ (CL 7, worth 1,700 gp — she was saving it for her own Wisdom damage), and her holy symbol of Zaneth (brass, worth 25 gp). She will trade the Scroll of Restoration for freedom and the party's promise to bring the Archive texts to the Oracle
    2. Cell 8 (the empty cell, Search DC 20): a loose stone in the floor conceals a small cache left by a previous prisoner — 120 gp, a _Potion of Invisibility_, and a crude map of Floor 3 scratched onto a copper plate (grants +2 on any Navigation or Knowledge checks related to Floor 3's layout)
    3. The corridor wall (Search DC 18): a maintenance panel concealing a narrow service passage that leads behind the cells. This passage connects to a ventilation shaft that accesses the Crucible Stair from above — allowing the party to approach Vokhul from an unexpected angle (surprise round possible, Spot DC 18 for Vokhul to notice them)
