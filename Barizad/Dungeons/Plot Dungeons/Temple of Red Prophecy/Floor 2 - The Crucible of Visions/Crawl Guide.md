# Temple of Red Prophecy (Floor 2) — Crawl Guide
*Levels 9–10 | EL 9–10 | Party of Four*

## General Features

**Construction Material.** Carved obsidian with copper-inlaid patterns — spirals, eyes, and the Chained Sun repeated at every junction. The walls are polished to a dark mirror finish, reflecting distorted versions of anyone who passes. The copper inlays glow faintly with residual heat from the volcanic rift. Construction is older here — pre-Aram-Zaneth stonework repurposed by the theocracy, with newer carvings layered over ancient ones.

**Ceiling Height.** Corridors are 10 feet high with arched ceilings. The Bloodletting Nave has a 40-foot vaulted ceiling above the altar pit. The Hall of Woven Smoke is 15 feet with heavy smoke pooling at the top. The Archive of Heresies has 12-foot ceilings with glyph-warded lintels. The Fever Cells are 8 feet — claustrophobic by design. The Crucible Stair is a 100-foot spiraling descent over an open shaft.

**Lighting.** Phosphorescent fungi and dying fire gutters provide dim light (shadowy illumination to 15 feet). The Bloodletting Nave is lit from below by the molten copper pit (bright light to 20 feet, shadowy to 40). The Archive of Heresies is dark — the wards suppress all light sources except daylight-level effects. The Fever Cells glow with the faint light of dreaming oracles (barely visible, provides no mechanical illumination).

## Crawl Strategy Notes

**Marching Order.** Corridors are 10 feet wide but the polished obsidian walls create confusing reflections — a party using light sources sees distorted doubles of themselves in the walls. This imposes no mechanical penalty but may cause momentary confusion (Spot DC 12 to distinguish reflection from reality in the first corridor). The Crucible Stair is 5 feet wide, forcing single-file.

**Visibility.** The ambient incense on Floor 2 imposes -1 on Will saves and -2 on Spot checks throughout. The Hall of Woven Smoke is worse (see room file). Light sources function normally except in the Archive of Heresies (suppressed). Darkvision is unimpeded by the incense but the reflective walls create visual noise that negates the advantage of Darkvision beyond 30 feet.

**Spot/Listen.** Note each PC's modifiers. Key thresholds:
- Spot +8 or better: distinguishes real movement from wall reflections without needing to make the DC 12 check each time
- Listen +10 or better: hears Sister Yussa's chanting in the Bloodletting Nave from two corridors away, giving the party time to prepare
- Spot +12 or better: notices the glyph wards on the Archive of Heresies' threshold before crossing them
- Listen +14 or better: hears Vokhul praying at the Crucible Stair — his voice carries up the shaft. The party knows he's there and can prepare

## Stretch Pacing

The five chambers are connected by spiraling corridors. Travelling between connected chambers costs 1 stretch per corridor. The floor can be navigated in multiple orders, but the Crucible Stair (leading to Floor 3) is at the deepest point.

### Room-by-Room Estimates

| Chamber | Estimated Stretches | Key Timing |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Corridors (between chambers) | 1 each | Roll for wandering encounters. Reflective obsidian walls. |
| The Hall of Woven Smoke (EL 9) | 2–4 | Vision puzzle requires 1–2 stretches of observation. Wrong choices cost 1 stretch each (trapped dead-ends). Will saves every stretch. |
| The Bloodletting Nave (EL 9) | 2–3 | Sister Yussa fight is round-scale. Examining the altar apparatus takes 1 stretch. The molten copper pit is a persistent environmental threat. |
| The Archive of Heresies (EL 10) | 3–5 | Disabling glyph wards takes 1 stretch per ward (3 wards). Reading the suppressed texts takes 2 stretches minimum. Fire traps trigger on failed checks. |
| The Fever Cells (EL 9) | 1–3 | Each cell can be investigated in 1 stretch. Psychic backlash from disturbing oracles is round-scale. Finding the captive informant requires checking 1d4+1 cells. |
| The Crucible Stair (EL 10) | 2–3 | Vokhul fight is round-scale on a spiraling staircase. The lava shaft below is a persistent falling hazard. |

**Total estimated stretches for Floor 2:** 14–24 (2.5–4 hours of in-game time)

### Relevant Activities (3.5e)

- **Search Area.** Critical in the Archive of Heresies (Search DC 20 for the hidden scrolls, DC 22 for the concealed vault) and the Fever Cells (Search DC 16 to identify which cell holds the captive informant without disturbing the oracles).
- **Keep Watch.** Important during extended reading in the Archive (wandering encounters) and while examining the Fever Cells (psychic backlash can incapacitate a party member).
- **Take Your Time.** Taking 20 on Disable Device checks for the Archive wards (DC 22) is possible but costs 2 stretches per ward and risks triggering wandering encounters. Taking 20 on Knowledge (religion) checks to interpret the suppressed texts is viable and recommended.
- **Helping Out.** Aid Another on Will saves is not possible (individual mental resistance). Aid Another on Search checks in the Archive reduces the time to find key texts.
- **Casting a Spell.** Ritual casting (10 min) fits into 1 stretch. Relevant rituals: Detect Magic (essential for identifying wards in the Archive), Remove Fear (for the Fever Cells' psychic backlash), Comprehend Languages (for reading the suppressed texts in their original Ignan).

## Wandering Monster Table

Wandering encounters on Floor 2 are stranger than Floor 1 — the incense and proximity to the dream-gem's influence produce encounters that blur the line between creature and vision. Some encounters may not be physically real.

**Encounter Frequency.** Medium — roll 1d6 at the end of every 3rd stretch.
**Encounter Likelihood.** Possible — on a 5 or 6, roll on the table.
**Encounter Difficulty.** Challenging — Floor 2 encounters are designed to drain Will saves and spell resources, softening the party for the Yussa and Vokhul fights.
