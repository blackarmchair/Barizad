1. **Description**

    1. A vast worship chamber — 60 feet across, 40 feet high — dominated by a massive altar suspended over a pit of molten copper. The altar is a slab of black basalt 10 feet long, carved with drainage channels that funnel blood into the pit below. Copper chains anchor it to the ceiling, and it sways faintly with the rift's thermal updrafts
    2. The pit is 30 feet in diameter, filled with molten copper that casts the entire chamber in shifting amber light. The heat is ferocious — exposed skin blisters at the edge. Narrow stone walkways (3 feet wide) ring the pit, connecting four entrances at the cardinal points
    3. Sister Yussa hovers 20 feet above the altar, wreathed in dark smoke, her Veil of Embers trailing like a banner. Before her on the altar sits a dream-gem divination apparatus: a brass armillary sphere with a small, dull crystal at its center — a fragment of a dream-gem, not the Hunger for Justice itself but a shard used for scrying
    4. Three priest-acolytes kneel on the walkway below, chanting in unison, their blood dripping from self-inflicted cuts on their forearms into the copper pit. The chanting echoes off the vaulted ceiling, creating a dissonant harmonic that vibrates in the bones

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **The Molten Copper Pit**: 30 feet diameter, 20 feet deep. Any creature that falls in takes 10d6 fire damage per round and is considered submerged (no air). The pit's edge is smooth basalt — Reflex DC 18 to catch the edge if pushed. The copper's glow provides bright light to 20 feet and shadowy illumination to 40 feet. Ranged attacks across the pit suffer -2 due to heat shimmer
    2. **The Walkways**: 3 feet wide, smooth stone. No Balance check required at normal speed. At full speed or while fighting: Balance DC 10 or fall prone. A creature that is bull rushed or trips on the walkway and falls prone must make a Reflex DC 14 save or slide into the pit. The walkways have no railings
    3. **The Suspended Altar**: Accessible by climbing the anchor chains (Climb DC 15, 20 feet up). The altar sways — any creature standing on it must make a Balance DC 12 check at the start of each round or move 5 feet in a random direction. The altar can hold 4 Medium creatures. Severing a chain (Hardness 10, 30 hp each, 4 chains) causes the altar to tilt — all creatures on it make Reflex DC 18 or fall into the pit. Severing two chains drops the altar entirely
    4. **The Chanting**: While two or more priest-acolytes are chanting, all creatures in the chamber take -2 on Will saves (stacks with Floor 2's ambient -1). Stopping the chant requires incapacitating or killing the chanters, or a successful Countersong (Perform DC 18). Silence also works but must cover all three acolytes
    5. **The Dream-Gem Apparatus**: The brass armillary sphere on the altar is a scrying device, not a weapon. Destroying it (Hardness 8, 20 hp) accomplishes nothing mechanically but enrages Yussa (she abandons tactical flight for reckless attacks, gaining +2 to hit but losing her Dark One's Own Luck for the rest of the fight). The shard inside is inert — it is spent, useful only as evidence that the Aram-Zaneth are actively scrying for dream-gems. A Knowledge (arcana) DC 16 check identifies what the apparatus does

3. **Encounters**

    1. **Sister Yussa, Mouth of Cinders** (Warlock 9, CR 9) — see stat block at `Barizad/Stat Blocks/Rival Parties/The Thirsting Light/Sister Yussa, Mouth of Cinders.md`. She fights from the air, exploiting the chamber's vertical geography
        1. _Tactics_: Yussa opens with Dark One's Own Luck, then drops Chilling Tentacles on a walkway to grapple and push melee fighters toward the pit. She uses Eldritch Spear (250 ft. range) from 30+ feet above the altar, well out of melee range. She targets healers with Frightful Blast to impose shaken, then focuses fire on anyone climbing the chains. If someone reaches her altitude, she uses Eldritch Cone at point-blank and retreats upward
        2. _Retreat_: Yussa flees below 25% hp (below 16 hp) unless Naehri is present. She flies toward the Crucible Stair to warn Vokhul. If she escapes, she joins Naehri on Floor 3
        3. _Speech of Ashes_: Her 10-foot aura deals 1 psychic damage per round (Will DC 13 negates). This punishes creatures that reach melee range — even if they climb the chains, fighting her at close range is painful
    2. 3 **Priest-Acolytes** (Cleric 3, CR 3 each) — kneeling on the walkway, chanting. They do not engage in combat unless attacked directly. If attacked, they fight defensively (total defense, AC 16) while continuing the chant. Each has: AC 13, hp 18, Will +5. They carry only ritual knives (daggers, 1d4) and holy symbols of Zaneth. Their purpose is the chanting — killing them is easy but raises the question of killing non-combatants who are actively powering an enemy
    3. If Thorrak escaped Floor 1, he is here in Wild Shape (Dire Wolf), flanking on the walkways. He reverts to cast Flame Strike if the party clusters on a single walkway

4. **Treasure**

    1. The dream-gem apparatus (if recovered intact, not destroyed): worth 2,000 gp as a scrying device to the right buyer. The spent shard is worth nothing monetarily but is plot-significant evidence of Aram-Zaneth dream-gem operations
    2. Yussa's equipment (if defeated): see stat block. Key items include the Veil of Embers, +1 Mithral Chain Shirt, Ring of Protection +2, Cloak of Charisma +2, and the Ash-sigiled Focus Stone
    3. The priest-acolytes' ritual space (Search DC 14): a brass coffer containing 3 _Potions of Cure Serious Wounds_ (CL 5), a _Scroll of Flame Strike_ (CL 9, worth 1,125 gp), and 250 gp in Zanethi coinage. The ritual knives are masterwork daggers (302 gp each)
    4. Beneath the altar (Search DC 20, requires climbing the chains): a hidden compartment containing a _Necklace of Fireballs_ (Type II, DMG pg. 263, worth 2,700 gp) — stored there by a previous occupant and forgotten
