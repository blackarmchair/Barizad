## Purpose

- Deepen the theological horror — the Aram-Zaneth's faith is sincere, fervent, and built on centuries of misinterpretation
- Introduce dream/vision mechanics that blur the line between what's real and what's prophetic, rewarding careful observation over brute force
- Reveal the key lore discovery: the Aram-Zaneth have never actually communicated with Zaneth — their "prophecies" are centuries of interpretation layered on interpretation
- Escalate the Thirsting Light threat with two boss encounters (Sister Yussa and Vokhul the Devoured)

## Themes

- Disorientation — prophetic visions intrude on reality, smoke and incense warp perception
- Theological doubt — the difference between faith and truth, revelation and delusion
- The architecture of belief — worship chambers, archives, isolation cells, all designed to produce visions and call them divine
- The Thirsting Light as true believers — they are not cynics exploiting the faithful but zealots who have gone deeper than anyone else

## Tone

Disorienting. Prophetic visions intrude on reality. The line between what's real and what's a vision blurs. Combat is complicated by environmental distortion — the party must solve coordination puzzles, interpret visions, and navigate spaces that do not behave consistently. The rooms demand mental saves more than physical ones.

## Level Range

- EL 9–10
- Designed for a party of four at levels 9–10

## Structure

Five chambers connected by spiraling corridors of carved obsidian. The corridors are lit by phosphorescent fungi and intermittent fire gutters. Incense is heavier here — the entire floor is saturated with narcotic smoke, imposing -1 on Will saves while on Floor 2.

1. The Hall of Woven Smoke (EL 9)
2. The Bloodletting Nave (EL 9)
3. The Archive of Heresies (EL 10)
4. The Fever Cells (EL 9)
5. The Crucible Stair (EL 10)
