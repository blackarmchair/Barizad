1. **Description**

    1. A spiraling staircase carved into the volcanic rock, descending 100 feet around a central shaft of open air. The shaft drops into darkness lit from far below by the volcanic glow of Floor 3. The stairs are 5 feet wide, cut from rough obsidian with no railing — the inner edge drops into the shaft, the outer wall is carved with Zanethi prayers in Ignan
    2. At the midpoint of the descent — 50 feet down — the staircase widens into a landing, 15 feet across. Here a figure kneels in prayer, facing the shaft, his massive form silhouetted against the volcanic glow below. Vokhul the Devoured. His ash-forged flail rests across his knees. His copper-scorched plate armor steams faintly
    3. The heat rising from the shaft creates powerful updrafts — loose objects flutter upward, cloaks billow, and the smoke from Floor 2 is drawn down and then pushed back up in swirling columns. The sound is a constant low roar, like standing inside a chimney
    4. Below the landing, the staircase continues another 50 feet down to a copper door — the entrance to Floor 3. The door is unsealed and warm to the touch

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **The Spiral Staircase**: 5 feet wide, no railing on the inner edge. Normal movement requires no check. Running, charging, or fighting on the stairs: Balance DC 12 or fall prone. A creature that falls prone on the inner edge must make a Reflex save (DC 14) or slide into the shaft. The stairs are steep — downhill movement grants +1 to melee attack rolls, uphill movement imposes -1
    2. **The Shaft**: 15-foot diameter, 200+ feet deep. A creature that falls into the shaft drops to Floor 3's ceiling level (100 feet, 10d6 falling damage) and must make a Reflex save (DC 18) to grab the staircase at that level — otherwise they fall into the volcanic rift below (200+ feet, effectively lethal). Feather Fall, Fly, or other falling-prevention effects work normally. The updraft reduces effective falling distance by 20 feet for creatures with large surface area (cloaks, wings)
    3. **The Landing**: 15 feet across, stable footing. The only space on the staircase where two Medium creatures can fight side by side. Vokhul has chosen this position deliberately — he can block the entire descent from here
    4. **Tight Quarters Combat**: On the 5-foot-wide stairs, creatures cannot flank (no room to get beside an opponent). Reach weapons are ineffective against targets on different elevation levels of the spiral (the curve blocks line of effect). Ranged attacks from above or below Vokhul suffer -2 due to the spiral's geometry (partial cover from the stair's curve)
    5. **Thermal Updraft**: Ranged attacks fired downward through the shaft suffer -2 (the updraft deflects projectiles). Ranged attacks fired upward gain +1 (the updraft assists). Alchemical fire dropped into the shaft detonates in the updraft, dealing 1d6 fire damage in a 10-foot radius at the point of ignition

3. **Encounters**

    1. **Vokhul the Devoured** (Barbarian 5/Paladin of Zaneth 4, CR 9) — see stat block at `Barizad/Stat Blocks/Rival Parties/The Thirsting Light/Vokhul the Devoured.md`. He fights with fanatical fury and will not retreat under any circumstances
        1. _Tactics_: Vokhul is praying when the party arrives. Unless surprised, he hears them coming (Listen +5, the staircase amplifies sound — DC 10 to hear footsteps from 50 feet). He stands, rages, and drinks his Potion of Haste. He activates Smite the Faithless and Divine Might on his first attack. He uses his position on the landing to force the party to come to him single-file on the stairs. He targets the first creature to reach him with his Pounce (if he has room to charge — 15 feet on the landing is enough for a short charge)
        2. _Fighting Style_: Vokhul uses the Ash-Forged Flail's reach to strike creatures on the stairs before they reach the landing. He uses Power Attack (-2/+4) by default and switches to -4/+8 against heavily armored targets. His Brass Tether prevents involuntary movement while raging — he cannot be bull rushed off the landing
        3. _Death_: Vokhul fights to the death. He does not negotiate, taunt, or monologue. He believes this is a sacred duty. His last words, if any, are: "The fire remembers." He falls into the shaft if killed on the landing's edge, and his body is retrievable at Floor 3's entrance (his equipment survives the fall due to the armor's heat resistance)
    2. If Sister Yussa escaped the Bloodletting Nave, she is here — flying in the shaft above the landing, providing air support with Eldritch Spear while Vokhul holds the stairs. This increases the EL to 12 and is extremely dangerous. She targets anyone casting spells from the stairs above

4. **Treasure**

    1. Vokhul's equipment (if defeated): see stat block. Key items include the Ash-Forged Flail +1 (+1d6 fire vs. dream-gem users or Lawful), Scorched Plate of Veneration +1, Amulet of Health +2, and Brass Tether
    2. Vokhul's prayer station on the landing (Search DC 14): a leather pack containing 4 _Potions of Cure Moderate Wounds_ (CL 3), a _Potion of Haste_ (if not consumed during the fight), 180 gp in Zanethi coinage, and a hand-copied prayer scroll that outlines the Thirsting Light's mission: "Recover the Hunger for Justice. Deliver it to the Voice at the Gate. Burn what cannot be carried." A Knowledge (local) or Knowledge (religion) DC 15 check identifies "the Voice at the Gate" as a reference to a high-ranking Aram-Zaneth operative outside the Temple
    3. Carved into the landing's surface (noticed automatically after combat): a crude map of Floor 3 scratched by Vokhul during his vigil, showing the positions of the Obsidian Nave, the Molten Font, and the Pyre Ascendant. This grants +2 on any checks to navigate Floor 3
