1. **Description**

    1. A sealed library carved into the volcanic rock — a long, narrow chamber (50 feet by 20 feet) lined with stone shelves holding scrolls, bound tablets, and sheets of hammered copper inscribed with text. Every shelf bears a small glyph ward: a stylized eye wreathed in flame. The air is dry and hot, preserved against moisture by the rift's thermal output
    2. The chamber's threshold is marked by three glyph wards in sequence — visible as faintly glowing copper inlays in the floor, spaced 5 feet apart. Beyond them, the light changes: fire gutters are absent here, replaced by a sourceless amber glow emanating from the copper-inscribed texts themselves
    3. The texts are organized by subject. The front shelves hold approved theology — commentaries on Zaneth's prophecies, liturgical calendars, ritual procedures. The deeper shelves, behind a locked iron grille, hold the suppressed material: accounts that contradict the official theology, sealed by order of the Council of Thorns
    4. The chamber smells of hot metal and old leather. The silence is absolute — the walls are thick enough to block the chanting from the Nave, creating an eerie contrast with the rest of Floor 2

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **Threshold Glyph Wards**: Three wards in sequence, spaced 5 feet apart. Each triggers independently when a creature crosses it without speaking the prayer of passage (Knowledge (religion) DC 20 to know, or learned from the Salamander patrols if they were persuaded):
        1. _First Ward_: Blast Glyph — 4d8 fire damage, Reflex DC 16 half. 5-foot radius
        2. _Second Ward_: Spell Glyph — _Blindness/Deafness_ (blindness), Fortitude DC 16 negates. Single target (first creature to cross)
        3. _Third Ward_: Spell Glyph — _Hold Person_, Will DC 16 negates, duration 5 rounds. Single target
    2. **Detecting and Disabling**: Search DC 28 to notice a ward (Detect Magic reveals all three automatically as moderate Abjuration). Disable Device DC 22 to disarm each ward (1 stretch per ward, or 10 rounds with haste). A ward can also be triggered safely by throwing an object across it (the blast ward fires, the spell wards target the object and fail)
    3. **The Iron Grille**: Bars the deeper shelves. Locked (Open Lock DC 20), Hardness 10, 40 hp. The key is held by the High Oracle on Floor 3. Forcing the grille open (Strength DC 25 or dealing 40 hp damage) makes significant noise — automatic wandering encounter check
    4. **The Suppressed Texts**: Behind the grille. Reading them requires Comprehend Languages (written in archaic Ignan) or Decipher Script DC 22 (2 stretches to read enough for the key revelation). The texts reveal:
        1. _Key lore discovery_: The Aram-Zaneth have never actually communicated with Zaneth. His "prophecies" are centuries of interpretation layered on interpretation. The earliest texts are dream-records from oracles who inhaled volcanic fumes — visions attributed to Zaneth but never confirmed by him. Each generation added commentary, treated commentary as scripture, and built theology on inference. The "divine revelation" of the Aram-Zaneth is an echo chamber extending back centuries
        2. _Secondary discovery_: The Celestine Furnace is referenced — the texts know it exists and know Zaneth was involved with it, but describe it as "the prison" rather than "the engine." The Aram-Zaneth believe the Furnace was built to imprison Zaneth, not that Zaneth sabotaged it
        3. _Tertiary discovery_: A reference to "the ten truths that fell from the Furnace's breaking" — the dream-gems. The author calls them "fragments of futures that should have been" and warns that "to hold one is to hold a verdict without appeal"
    5. **Fire Traps on Specific Texts**: Three texts in the suppressed section are additionally trapped — the most heretical accounts are warded against reading. Touching a trapped text triggers Burning Hands (3d4 fire, Reflex DC 13 half, 15-foot cone from the text). Search DC 20 to notice the trap before triggering, Disable Device DC 20 to disarm

3. **Encounters**

    1. 1 **Archive Guardian** (use Invisible Stalker, MM pg. 160, CR 7) — bound to the chamber by the Council of Thorns. It manifests if anyone opens the iron grille or touches a suppressed text. It fights to incapacitate (nonlethal damage if possible — it grapples and pins, using its improved grab and Str 18). It can be dismissed with a Dismissal spell (CL check DC 18) or by speaking the prayer of passage while holding a holy symbol of Zaneth
    2. 2 **Glyph Sentinels** (use Animated Object, Medium, MM pg. 14, CR 2 each) — copper-inscribed stone tablets that animate if the grille is forced open. They attack with slam (+5 melee, 1d6+3) and have Hardness 5. They pursue intruders through the Archive but do not leave the chamber

4. **Treasure**

    1. The approved theology section (freely accessible): religious texts worth 400 gp total to a scholar, a _Scroll of Resist Energy_ (CL 7, fire, worth 700 gp) used as a bookmark, and a _Phylactery of Faithfulness_ (DMG pg. 263, worth 1,000 gp) left on a reading stand
    2. The suppressed section (behind the grille): the heretical texts are priceless to the right buyer — a collection worth 3,000 gp to Myrian scholars, Varnathi historians, or anyone researching the true history of Zaneth and the Celestine Furnace. They are also plot-critical: presenting them to the High Oracle on Floor 3 shifts the encounter from hostile to philosophical
    3. A concealed vault behind the deepest shelf (Search DC 22, requires moving the shelf — Strength DC 16): a _Ring of Spell Storing_ (minor, DMG pg. 233, worth 18,000 gp) containing Shield of Faith (CL 6, +3 deflection, 6 minutes) and Resist Energy (Fire, CL 7, resist 20, 70 minutes). This was hidden by a heretic priest who planned to use it to escape the Temple and never returned for it
