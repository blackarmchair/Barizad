1. **Description**

    1. A long corridor — 80 feet long, 15 feet wide — where the incense is so thick it has taken on texture and form. The smoke does not drift — it *weaves*, forming shapes that dissolve and reform: faces, cities, battles, symbols. The walls are polished obsidian, turning the smoke-shapes into an infinite regress of burning reflections
    2. The corridor branches at the midpoint into three passages: left, center, and right. Each passage is 30 feet long and ends at a copper door. Only one door leads forward to the Bloodletting Nave. The other two are trapped dead-ends
    3. Above the branching point, the smoke coalesces into three distinct visions — sustained, repeating images that play out on a loop. Each vision depicts a possible future. One is true. The others are fabrications generated by the Temple's prophetic infrastructure
    4. The floor is warm basalt, inscribed with concentric circles radiating from the branching point. The inscriptions are prayers in Ignan, worn almost smooth by centuries of prostrating worshippers

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **The Three Visions**: At the branching point, the smoke forms three visions, each 5 feet across, hovering at eye level:
        1. _Left Vision_: A burning city — towers of brass collapsing into a sea of fire. Figures in Zanethi robes stand at the edge, arms raised in triumph. **This is a fabrication.** Knowledge (religion) DC 18 recognizes the imagery as standard Zanethi eschatology — generic prophecy, not a true vision
        2. _Center Vision_: A dark chamber. A figure sits on a throne of fused obsidian and copper, drinking from a basin of liquid brass. The figure looks up — directly at the viewer — and its eyes are holes burning with white fire. **This is true.** It depicts the High Oracle on Floor 3. A Sense Motive DC 16 or Spellcraft DC 18 detects that this vision has a different *quality* — sharper, more coherent, resonating with actual prophetic energy
        3. _Right Vision_: A party of adventurers (resembling the PCs but subtly wrong — different gear, different expressions) standing victorious over a slain dragon in a brass cavern. **This is a fabrication.** Spot DC 15 notices the inconsistencies (wrong equipment, wrong number of party members)
    2. **Choosing a Path**: The correct door is the one matching the true vision (center). Choosing wrong:
        1. _Left door_: The passage ends at a wall. When the door closes behind the party (automatically, 1 round after entry), a Glyph of Warding triggers: Blast Glyph (5d8 fire, Reflex DC 16 half). The door can be forced back open (Strength DC 18)
        2. _Right door_: The passage ends at a wall covered in mirrors. Each mirror shows a party member committing an act of betrayal. Will DC 16 or shaken for 1 hour. The door does not lock but swings shut slowly (1 round to exit)
        3. _Center door_: Opens to a short corridor leading to the Bloodletting Nave. No trap
    3. **Alternative Navigation**: A party that ignores the visions entirely can:
        1. Search all three passages (1 stretch each, Search DC 14 to determine dead-end vs. passage without opening the door)
        2. Use Detect Magic to identify the trapped doors (Abjuration aura on left, Illusion aura on right, no aura on center)
        3. Break through the dead-end walls (Hardness 8, 60 hp each) — this works but takes 2 stretches and makes significant noise (automatic wandering encounter)
    4. **Ambient Will Saves**: The smoke in this corridor is concentrated prophetic incense. Will DC 15 each stretch or the creature receives a vision: a flash of something personal — a memory, a fear, a desire — that the DM should tailor to the character. Mechanically, a failed save imposes -2 on the character's next attack roll or skill check (distraction). These visions are not true prophecy — they are the Temple's architecture *trying* to initiate the viewer

3. **Encounters**

    1. 2 **Vision-Phantasms** (use Allip stats, MM pg. 10, CR 3 each, but aberration with incorporeal subtype, not undead) — semi-physical prophetic manifestations that emerge from the smoke when a creature fails a Will save. They appear as burning silhouettes of figures from the failed creature's vision. Their touch drains Wisdom (1d4 Wisdom damage, Will DC 15 negates). Destroyed on any hit from a force or fire effect, or when the creature that spawned them succeeds on a Will save (DC 15) as a standard action to "reject the vision"
    2. If the party spends more than 4 stretches in the corridor, the smoke coalesces into a **Large Incense Elemental** (as Large Air Elemental, MM pg. 95, CR 5, with modified attacks: slam deals 1d6 + Fort DC 14 or nauseated 1 round, whirlwind forces Fort DC 14 or confused 1d4 rounds) — the Temple's immune response to creatures that linger too long

4. **Treasure**

    1. The left dead-end (after the Glyph triggers or is disabled, Disable Device DC 28): a brass offering box bolted to the wall containing 320 gp in Zanethi coinage and a _Pearl of Power_ (1st level, DMG pg. 263, worth 1,000 gp)
    2. The right dead-end mirrors (Search DC 20): one mirror is hinged. Behind it: a _Scroll of Dispel Magic_ (CL 5, worth 375 gp), a _Potion of Remove Blindness/Deafness_, and a journal written by a previous explorer who made it this far and turned back. The journal describes the Bloodletting Nave's layout and Sister Yussa's presence (granting the party foreknowledge of the encounter if they read it — 1 stretch)
    3. The floor inscriptions (Knowledge (arcana) or Spellcraft DC 18 to recognize): the concentric circles are a massive, dormant magic circle. It could theoretically be activated to suppress all Illusion effects on Floor 2 for 1 hour (requires a 5th-level spell slot channeled into the inscription as a full-round action). This would eliminate the smoke visions, the wall reflections, and the phantasms — but also alert every creature on Floor 2 to the disruption
