1. **Description**

    1. A circular chamber, 40 feet in diameter, built around a natural fissure in the volcanic rift. At its center: a basin of liquid brass, 10 feet across, glowing white-gold. The basin is carved from a single block of obsidian, its rim inlaid with copper script so old the words have been half-consumed by heat
    2. Behind the basin, facing the entrance, sits a throne of fused obsidian and copper — grown from the chamber's floor rather than built. On the throne sits the High Oracle. She is ancient, burned, barely human. Her skin is black and cracked like cooling lava, seamed with lines of dull red where heat still flows. Her eyes are copper-bright, unblinking. Her lips are permanently parted, stained brass-gold from centuries of drinking from the font
    3. The Oracle's breath creates small gouts of brass vapor. The air around her throne shimmers with heat. She has not moved from this position in decades. Copper tubing runs from the basin to the throne's armrests — a direct feed. She drinks constantly
    4. The chamber's walls are natural volcanic glass, uncarved, unfinished. This is not a constructed space — it is a natural formation that the Temple was built around. The Oracle sits at the geological and theological center of everything

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **The Molten Brass Basin**: The liquid brass is at approximately 1,800°F. Contact deals 12d6 fire damage per round. Splashing brass (from combat, tremors, or deliberate action) deals 4d6 fire damage in a 10-foot radius, Reflex DC 16 half. The basin radiates severe heat: Fortitude DC 15 every round within 5 feet, or 1d4 nonlethal fire damage. Creatures with fire resistance reduce this normally. The Oracle is immune
    2. **The Oracle's Presence**: The High Oracle radiates an aura of prophetic weight. Within 20 feet: all creatures must make a Will save (DC 18) when they first enter the aura. Failure: the creature sees a flash of its own most likely death — vivid, specific, and terrifying. Shaken for 1d4 hours. Success: the creature feels the weight of possible futures pressing against its mind but resists. This is not a spell effect — it is the accumulated prophetic energy of centuries of brass-drinking. It cannot be dispelled, but Death Ward suppresses it
    3. **Roleplay Encounter — The Oracle**: The High Oracle (Cleric 11, Zaneth — Fire/Knowledge domains, CR 11) is not hostile by default. She speaks slowly, in a voice that resonates with metallic harmonics. She knows the party is here. She has seen this moment in a thousand variations
        1. _She can be interrogated_: She will answer questions truthfully. She knows the truth about Zaneth — that he is indifferent to his worshippers, that the Aram-Zaneth's prophecies are self-generated, that the faith is built on centuries of interpretation. She considers this irrelevant: "The fire speaks regardless of whether we hear it correctly. Zaneth did not ask to be worshipped. We did not ask to need him. Both things are true and neither matters"
        2. _She can be bargained with_: She will trade information for the party's memories. Literally — she can extract a memory through the brass (Will DC 20 to resist if unwilling). She offers: the location of the dream-gem, the layout of the Reliquary's traps, or the truth about the Thirsting Light's mission. Each piece of information costs one memory. The lost memory is a personal one (the DM chooses — a childhood moment, a mentor's face, the sound of a loved one's voice). The memory is not destroyed; it goes into the brass, where the Oracle can experience it
        3. _She can be fought_: See Encounters below. This is extremely dangerous in her sanctum
        4. _If the party presents evidence from the Archive of Heresies_ (the suppressed texts): The Oracle reads them without surprise. "I wrote half of these. I sealed them myself. The truth does not need to be public to be true." This shifts the encounter from transactional to philosophical. She becomes more forthcoming, willing to discuss Zaneth's actual nature, the Celestine Furnace, and the dream-gems without demanding memory-payment. She will not help the party against Naehri ("She believes as I once believed. She is wrong, but she is sincere. I will not betray sincerity")
        5. _If the party presents the theological keystone tablet_ from the Brass Spiral: The Oracle holds it for a long moment. "I have not seen this in forty years." She becomes almost gentle. She offers to voluntarily disable the Reliquary's traps — a full-round action of concentration that deactivates all wards and suppresses the construct guardian for 1 hour. This is the optimal outcome
    4. **Fighting the Oracle**: If the party attacks or if negotiation fails catastrophically (Diplomacy check below 5, or desecrating the font):
        1. The Oracle fights from the throne. She does not stand — she does not need to. She casts spells while drinking brass (no somatic components required — she has modified her casting through decades of practice)
        2. Pre-cast buffs (always active): Shield of Faith (+3 deflection), Magic Vestment (+3 armor), Freedom of Movement
        3. Combat spells: Flame Strike (10d6, half fire/half divine), Searing Light (ranged touch, 5d8 vs. undead/3d6 otherwise), Dispel Magic, Silence (targeted on casters), Hold Person (Will DC 18)
        4. She can command the basin — as a standard action, she causes brass to erupt in a 15-foot cone from the basin (6d6 fire, Reflex DC 18 half)
        5. Stats: AC 24 (natural armor from decades of brass consumption + buffs), hp 82, Fort +11, Ref +5, Will +15. She has Spell Resistance 16 (brass-infused body)

3. **Encounters**

    1. **The High Oracle** (Cleric 11 of Zaneth, CR 11) — see Mechanics for combat statistics and roleplay options. She is the most dangerous creature in the dungeon if fought on her throne. She is also the most valuable source of information if engaged diplomatically
    2. 2 **Oracle-Sentinels** (Cleric 4 of Zaneth, CR 4 each) — standing flanking the entrance, silent and still. They do not engage in conversation. They attack only if the Oracle is attacked first, or if commanded. They cast Hold Person (Will DC 15) and Spiritual Weapon. AC 18, hp 30 each

4. **Treasure**

    1. The Oracle does not carry conventional treasure. Her value is informational. If the party defeats her (or if she dies — she is ancient and fragile beneath the brass), her throne contains: 2,000 gp worth of fused copper and brass (requires 2 stretches to extract), a _Periapt of Wisdom +4_ (DMG pg. 263, worth 16,000 gp) embedded in the throne's headrest, and a copper key that opens the Reliquary directly (bypassing all traps)
    2. The basin itself: the liquid brass is valuable (approximately 5,000 gp worth of raw brass if cooled and extracted — requires *control water* or similar, and several days of cooling). The basin's rim inscription (Knowledge (arcana) DC 20 to read): a formula for brewing the Oracle's brass sacrament. A character who drinks from the font gains permanent fire resistance 5 but takes 8d6 fire damage from the initial drink (Fortitude DC 20 for half) and permanently loses 2 points of Charisma (the skin cracks and darkens)
    3. The Oracle-Sentinels carry: masterwork heavy maces (312 gp each), _+1 breastplates_ (1,350 gp each), and holy symbols of Zaneth
