1. **Description**

    1. A labyrinthine chamber shaped like the sacred Brass Spiral glyph — the central symbol of Zanethi cosmology. The floor plan is a logarithmic spiral, with walls of polished obsidian rising 15 feet to a mirrored ceiling that reflects the spiral back on itself. The effect is vertigo-inducing: the path seems to extend infinitely inward
    2. The spiral walls are carved with text — Ignan scripture, philosophical argument, theological debate — centuries of Zanethi thought inscribed in concentric layers. The earliest inscriptions are at the spiral's outermost curve (archaic, fragmented). The innermost inscriptions are recent (formal, dogmatic). The progression from outside to inside mirrors the theology's evolution from uncertainty to certainty
    3. At the spiral's center: a small circular chamber, 15 feet in diameter, containing a brass lectern holding a single copper tablet. This is the theological keystone — the oldest surviving text in the Temple's possession
    4. The spiral shifts. The walls move when not directly observed — passages that were open close, dead-ends open into new routes. The chamber is a living puzzle, reconfigured by the Temple's prophetic infrastructure to test those who enter

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **Navigation Puzzle**: The spiral reconfigures between stretches (or whenever the entire party is not looking — such as during combat or while reading inscriptions). The party must navigate from the outer edge to the center.
        1. _Intelligence or Wisdom check (DC 16)_ each stretch to make progress toward the center. Success: the party advances one ring inward (3 rings total from entrance to center). Failure: the party is looped back to their current position and loses 1 stretch
        2. _Alternative approaches_: Detect Magic reveals the reconfiguration mechanism (moderate Transmutation on the walls). Dispel Magic (CL check DC 18) freezes the walls for 10 minutes (1 stretch), allowing free navigation. Stone Shape can create permanent openings (the walls are obsidian, not true stone — the spell works but the opening is rough and 5 feet maximum). Flight over the 15-foot walls works but the mirrored ceiling creates vertigo (Will DC 14 or confused for 1 round upon looking up)
        3. _The walls do not move if someone is touching them._ A party that keeps hands on both walls simultaneously can navigate without checks — but this means single-file with both hands occupied (no weapons, no spellcasting with somatic components). This is the intended pilgrimage method
    2. **The Inscriptions**: Reading the wall inscriptions reveals the theological evolution:
        1. _Outer ring_ (Comprehend Languages or Decipher Script DC 18): Fragmentary records of volcanic dreams. "The fire spoke but I did not understand." "A shape in the caldera — was it Zaneth or my own fear?" These are the earliest "prophecies" — personal experiences, unmediated
        2. _Middle ring_ (Comprehend Languages or Decipher Script DC 16): Formal theology emerging. "Zaneth spoke through the fire and said: all chains are prisons." Commentary on the outer texts, treating uncertainties as certainties. The interpretive layer is clearly visible
        3. _Inner ring_ (readable in Common — the formal doctrine is accessible): Dogmatic proclamations. "The Father of Flame has decreed." "The Thirsting Court demands." These read as absolute truth but a Knowledge (religion) DC 14 check recognizes them as derivative — commentary on commentary, not primary revelation
    3. **The Theological Keystone**: The copper tablet on the lectern at the center. Written in proto-Ignan (Comprehend Languages required, or Decipher Script DC 24). It reads: "I stood at the furnace and asked it for the future. It showed me fire. I asked what the fire meant. It showed me more fire. I called the fire Zaneth because I needed it to have a name." This is the foundational text of the Aram-Zaneth faith — and it is an admission that the entire theology is an act of naming, not revelation
    4. **Removing the Tablet**: The tablet can be taken (not trapped, not warded). Doing so causes the spiral to freeze permanently — the walls stop moving. This is narratively significant: removing the keystone removes the Temple's theological foundation. The tablet is worth 5,000 gp to scholars but its true value is as evidence when confronting the Oracle

3. **Encounters**

    1. 1 **Spiral Guardian** (use Shield Guardian, MM pg. 223, CR 8, but made of obsidian and brass rather than stone and iron — same statistics. Its amulet is fused into the lectern at the center) — activates if any creature reaches the center chamber without completing the navigation puzzle legitimately (i.e., by breaking walls, flying over, or using Dispel Magic to freeze the walls). It attacks to drive creatures away from the lectern. If the party navigated honestly (3 successful checks), the guardian remains inert
    2. The spiral's reconfiguration can separate party members. If the party is split across different rings when the walls shift, each group must make separate navigation checks. A separated party member who fails a Will save (DC 14) hears whispered accusations from the walls — the dream-gem's influence, amplified by the spiral's geometry. This is unsettling but not damaging

4. **Treasure**

    1. The theological keystone tablet (if taken): worth 5,000 gp to Myrian scholars, Varnathi historians, or anyone studying the Aram-Zaneth. Plot-significant: presenting it to the High Oracle forces a pivotal roleplay moment
    2. The wall inscriptions (not removable — carved into obsidian): a party with Comprehend Languages and time (3 stretches) can transcribe enough to produce a document worth 1,500 gp to religious scholars. The transcription also grants permanent +2 on Knowledge (religion) checks related to Zaneth, the Aram-Zaneth, and the Celestine Furnace (knowledge bonus, not an item)
    3. The lectern's base (Search DC 18): a hidden drawer containing 3 _Scrolls of Divination_ (CL 7, worth 700 gp each) and a _Headband of Intellect +2_ (DMG pg. 258, worth 4,000 gp) — used by temple scholars navigating the spiral. The headband is brass, inscribed with Zanethi prayers
