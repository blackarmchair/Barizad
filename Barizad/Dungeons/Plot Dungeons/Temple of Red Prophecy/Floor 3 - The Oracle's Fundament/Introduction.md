## Purpose

- Deliver the dungeon's climax: the High Oracle, the dream-gem, and Naehri's final stand
- Provide the party with the truth about Zaneth — not through revelation but through confrontation with someone who already knows and has chosen to continue anyway
- Force a decision: take the dream-gem, bargain with the Oracle, or confront Naehri — potentially all three
- The ambient effects of the Hunger for Justice (the dream-gem) pervade this floor, making every interaction emotionally charged

## Themes

- Dread and grandeur — the volcanic rift is open below, reality warps near the Oracle
- The cost of truth — the Oracle knows the Aram-Zaneth's faith is built on misinterpretation and considers this fact irrelevant
- Justice without mercy — the dream-gem's influence compels confession, surfaces grudges, and demands that someone pay
- Cosmic indifference — Zaneth does not care about his worshippers, the Oracle does not care about the truth, and the fire burns regardless

## Tone

Dread and grandeur. The volcanic rift is open below. Reality warps near the Oracle. This floor should feel like standing at the edge of something cosmic — the party is deep inside a functioning theological engine, and the engine does not care whether its theology is true. Every room should carry the weight of the dream-gem's influence: old grudges surfacing between party members, a compulsion to confess, the sense that *someone must pay*.

## Level Range

- EL 10–12
- Designed for a party of four at level 10

## Structure

Five chambers arranged around the deepest point of the volcanic rift. The Obsidian Nave is the entry point. The Molten Font and the Reliquary can be approached in either order. The Pyre Ascendant is the final chamber — Naehri does not allow the party to leave without a confrontation.

1. The Obsidian Nave (EL 10)
2. The Chamber of the Brass Spiral (EL 10)
3. The Molten Font (EL 11)
4. The Reliquary (EL 10)
5. The Pyre Ascendant (EL 12)
