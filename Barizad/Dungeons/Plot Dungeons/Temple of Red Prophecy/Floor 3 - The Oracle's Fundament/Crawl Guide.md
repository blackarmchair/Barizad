# Temple of Red Prophecy (Floor 3) — Crawl Guide
*Level 10 | EL 10–12 | Party of Four*

## General Features

**Construction Material.** Volcanic glass and living rock. The walls are natural obsidian — not hewn but grown, pushed upward by geological forces and shaped by centuries of ritual carving. Copper veins run through the stone like arteries, pulsing with heat. Brass fixtures are fused directly into the rock. The architecture is half-temple, half-geode — the deeper the party goes, the less it looks like something built and the more it looks like something *grown*.

**Ceiling Height.** The Obsidian Nave is cathedral-scale: 50 feet to the vaulted ceiling of volcanic glass. The Chamber of the Brass Spiral is 15 feet with a labyrinthine ceiling that mirrors the floor's spiral pattern. The Molten Font is 30 feet with a natural chimney venting heat upward. The Reliquary is 12 feet, sealed tight. The Pyre Ascendant is open to the volcanic rift above — effectively no ceiling, just the shaft rising 200 feet to Floor 2 and beyond.

**Lighting.** The volcanic rift provides constant ambient light — deep orange-red, flickering, casting everything in sharp relief with deep shadows. The Obsidian Nave is lit by the rift's glow reflected through the glass walls (bright light, but tinted red — colors are distorted). The Reliquary is the only dark room on this floor (sealed and lightless). The Pyre Ascendant is lit from below by open magma.

## Crawl Strategy Notes

**Marching Order.** Corridors between chambers are 10 feet wide but the obsidian floor is uneven — occasional cracks reveal the rift below (no falling hazard, but vertigo-inducing). The Brass Spiral forces single-file and reconfigures — the party should establish a lead navigator. The Pyre Ascendant is an open arena.

**Visibility.** The red ambient light means all creatures can see without light sources but suffer -2 on Spot checks for fine detail (reading text, identifying expressions at distance). The dream-gem's ambient influence creates visual distortions: mirrors in the Obsidian Nave show things that aren't there, the Brass Spiral seems to shift when not directly observed. These are not illusions and cannot be dispelled.

**Spot/Listen.** Note each PC's modifiers. Key thresholds:
- Spot +10 or better: notices the cracking floor sections in the Obsidian Nave before stepping on them
- Listen +10 or better: hears the Oracle's murmuring in the Molten Font from two rooms away — an unsettling drone of half-words
- Spot +14 or better: notices the construct guardian in the Reliquary before it activates (surprise round for the party)
- Listen +16 or better: hears Naehri's preparations in the Pyre Ascendant — spell-casting, the clink of armor, whispered prayers. The party knows she's ready for them

## Stretch Pacing

The five chambers radiate from a central junction near the volcanic rift's deepest accessible point. Travelling between connected chambers costs 1 stretch. The Brass Spiral takes longer to navigate (see room file).

### Room-by-Room Estimates

| Chamber | Estimated Stretches | Key Timing |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Corridors (between chambers) | 1 each | Roll for wandering encounters. Dream-gem influence: Will DC 14 or surface a personal grudge (roleplay, no mechanical penalty). |
| The Obsidian Nave (EL 10) | 2–3 | Combat with elite guards is round-scale. Investigating reflections takes 1 stretch. Cracking floor sections add urgency. |
| The Chamber of the Brass Spiral (EL 10) | 3–5 | Navigation puzzle requires 2–3 stretches minimum. Wrong turns cost 1 stretch each. The theological keystone takes 1 stretch to study. |
| The Molten Font (EL 11) | 2–4 | Roleplay encounter with the Oracle: 1–3 stretches depending on the party's approach. Combat (if it occurs) is round-scale and extremely dangerous. |
| The Reliquary (EL 10) | 2–3 | Trap detection and disabling: 1–2 stretches. Construct fight is round-scale. Retrieving the dream-gem takes 1 stretch. |
| The Pyre Ascendant (EL 12) | 1–2 | The final fight. Round-scale combat. Naehri has full preparation. |

**Total estimated stretches for Floor 3:** 14–22 (2.5–4 hours of in-game time)

### Relevant Activities (3.5e)

- **Search Area.** Critical in the Reliquary (Search DC 24 for traps) and the Obsidian Nave (Search DC 18 for safe floor paths). The Brass Spiral rewards Intelligence checks over Search checks.
- **Keep Watch.** Important during the Oracle encounter (the party is vulnerable during roleplay) and while navigating the Brass Spiral (the labyrinth can separate party members).
- **Take Your Time.** Taking 20 on Disable Device checks in the Reliquary (DC 24) is possible but costs 2 stretches and risks the construct activating mid-work. Taking 20 on Knowledge checks in the Brass Spiral is recommended.
- **Helping Out.** Aid Another on Diplomacy checks with the Oracle is possible and valuable — the Oracle responds to earnest engagement. Aid Another on navigation checks in the Brass Spiral is the intended design.
- **Casting a Spell.** Ritual casting (10 min) fits into 1 stretch. Relevant rituals: Detect Magic (the dream-gem radiates overwhelming divination), Dispel Magic (useful against the Reliquary wards), Freedom of Movement (valuable for the Pyre Ascendant fight against Naehri's Hold Person).

## Wandering Monster Table

Wandering encounters on Floor 3 reflect the dream-gem's influence and the rift's proximity. The encounters are fewer but more dangerous. Some are not creatures at all but psychic phenomena.

**Encounter Frequency.** Medium — roll 1d6 at the end of every 3rd stretch.
**Encounter Likelihood.** Possible — on a 5 or 6, roll on the table.
**Encounter Difficulty.** Severe — Floor 3 encounters can threaten a depleted party. They are designed to force the party to commit to their final approach: Oracle first, Reliquary first, or straight to Naehri.
