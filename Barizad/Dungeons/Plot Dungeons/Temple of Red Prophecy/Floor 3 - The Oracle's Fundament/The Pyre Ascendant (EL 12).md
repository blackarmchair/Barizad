1. **Description**

    1. The summit of the Temple's ritual architecture — a circular platform, 50 feet in diameter, carved from the volcanic rift's lip. The platform is open to the shaft above (rising 200 feet through Floor 2 to Floor 1 and the sky beyond) and to the rift below (a 30-foot drop to churning magma). There are no walls. There is no ceiling. The platform is an island of stone in a column of superheated air
    2. A ring of copper braziers marks the platform's edge, each one burning with sacred fire. Between the braziers, the drop is unguarded. The platform's surface is inscribed with the Brass Spiral in copper inlay — the same glyph from the Chamber below, but here it is 50 feet across and glowing with residual heat
    3. At the center of the spiral, Naehri of the Thron-Dream stands in full ritual regalia. Her Red-Soot Robes billow in the thermal updraft. Her eyes — or rather, the spaces where her eyes were before she blinded herself — glow with the faint amber of dream-sight. She holds her Hierophant's Staff in one hand. In the other, the Censer of Smothering Insight trails dark smoke that curls against the updraft
    4. She has been waiting. She has been praying. She is not surprised

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **The Platform**: 50-foot diameter circle. The edge drops 30 feet to magma (10d6 fire damage per round, effectively lethal). A creature pushed off the edge may attempt a Reflex save (DC 14) to grab the platform's lip. Climbing back up requires a Climb check (DC 12) and provokes attacks of opportunity from adjacent enemies. The braziers (5 feet tall, brass) provide cover (+4 AC) and can be bull rushed into enemies (Strength check to push a brazier: DC 16, deals 2d6 fire damage on contact)
    2. **Thermal Updraft**: The column of superheated air rising from the rift creates a powerful updraft. Flying creatures must make a Fly check (DC 15) at the start of each turn to maintain position — failure means they rise 10 feet involuntarily. Falling creatures have their fall reduced by 20 feet (minimum 0). Ranged attacks suffer -2 (crosswinds and heat distortion). The updraft carries sound upward — combat on the platform can be heard from Floor 1
    3. **The Brass Spiral Inscription**: The spiral glows when Naehri casts divine spells. When it glows:
        1. All creatures standing on the spiral (which covers most of the platform) take 1d4 fire damage per round (no save — the floor is heated)
        2. Naehri gains +2 sacred bonus to caster level for divine spells while standing on the spiral
        3. The glow can be suppressed by Dispel Magic (CL check DC 18, suppressed for 1d4 rounds) or by pouring water/cold effects on the spiral (suppressed for 1 round per 10 points of cold damage dealt to the floor)
    4. **Naehri's Preparations**: Naehri has cast the following spells before the party arrives (assume she had at least 10 minutes of warning from the Temple's alarm systems):
        1. Shield of Faith (+3 deflection AC)
        2. Magic Vestment (+2 armor bonus to her robes)
        3. Freedom of Movement
        4. Prayer (all allies +1 luck, all enemies -1 luck, while Naehri maintains concentration)
    5. **Rally**: If any Thirsting Light members survived earlier floors, they are here. Each surviving member adds to the encounter:
        1. _Thorrak_ (CR 9): fights in Wild Shape (Dire Wolf), flanking with Vokhul. Casts Flame Strike and Call Lightning
        2. _Sister Yussa_ (CR 9): flies above the platform using Eldritch Spear. Targets spellcasters. Her Speech of Ashes aura damages melee fighters who reach her
        3. _Vokhul_ (CR 9): rages and charges the most martial-looking party member. Uses Smite + Divine Might + Pounce for a devastating opening
        4. _Full rally EL_: Naehri alone = EL 12. Each additional member adds approximately +1 to EL (Naehri + 1 = EL 13, Naehri + 2 = EL 14, Naehri + 3 = EL 15 — this last scenario should be near-impossible if the party fought through Floors 1 and 2)

3. **Encounters**

    1. **Naehri of the Thron-Dream** (Cleric 6/Hierarch of Zaneth 3, CR 9) — see stat block at `Barizad/Stat Blocks/Rival Parties/The Thirsting Light/Naehri of the Thron-Dream.md`. She fights with full preparation and absolute conviction
        1. _Tactics_: Naehri opens with Obscuring Mist or Silence (centered on enemy casters). She uses Discordant Chant to prevent readied/delayed actions (Will DC 17). She targets Lawful PCs with Hold Person (Will DC 18, Liturgical Schism imposes -2 on saves for Lawful targets within 10 feet). She follows up with Searing Light on held targets. If multiple enemies close to melee range, she uses Sound Burst (Fort DC 16 or stunned 1 round) and her Hierophant's Staff in melee (+7, 1d6+2)
        2. _Discordant Chant_: While chanting (a move action each round to maintain), all enemies within 30 feet cannot ready or delay actions. Will DC 17 at the start of each turn to resist. This fundamentally changes action economy — the party must commit to their actions without being able to react to Naehri's spells
        3. _Inversion Doctrine_: When Naehri casts a confusion or illusion spell, she can sacrifice a spell slot to force the target to reroll a successful save. This means even high-Will characters may fail once
        4. _Iconoclast's Rebuttal_: If a creature targets Naehri with a mind-affecting effect, she can force the source to make a Will save (DC 17) or be confused for 1d4 rounds. This punishes Enchantment-focused casters
        5. _Death_: Naehri does not surrender. If reduced below 0 hp, she spends her dying round attempting to cast one final Flame Strike centered on herself — a martyrdom. If this kills a party member, it is a moment of genuine horror. Her last words: "The fire does not need me. But I needed the fire"
    2. Any surviving Thirsting Light members (see Rally above). Their tactics interlock with Naehri's:
        1. Vokhul holds the melee line while Naehri casts from behind
        2. Yussa provides air support and targets anyone Naehri has debuffed
        3. Thorrak summons creatures to clog the platform and uses battlefield control to prevent retreat

4. **Treasure**

    1. Naehri's equipment (if defeated): see stat block. Key items include the Red-Soot Robes of the Oracle (+2 deflection AC), Hierophant's Staff +1, Periapt of Wisdom +2, and Censer of Smothering Insight
    2. Naehri carries (on her person): the Thirsting Light's mission orders — a copper scroll sealed with the Chained Sun, detailing their objectives ("Recover the Hunger for Justice from the Reliquary. Deliver it to Issar-Zul at the Gate of Brass. Eliminate witnesses. The Oracle is not to be harmed — her visions still serve"). This connects the Thirsting Light to Issar-Zul and the broader Aram-Zaneth conspiracy
    3. The braziers (8 total): each contains a core of _Alchemist's Fire_ (worth 20 gp each) and a copper offering bowl with 1d20+10 gp in Zanethi coinage. One brazier (Search DC 18) has a hidden compartment in its base containing a _Wand of Cure Moderate Wounds_ (22 charges, worth 1,980 gp) — Naehri's emergency healing reserve
    4. If the party recovers equipment from all four Thirsting Light members across the dungeon, the combined haul includes significant wealth: the total value of unique items across all four stat blocks exceeds 40,000 gp. This is appropriate for the dungeon's level range and the difficulty of fighting a CR 9 rival party four times
