1. **Description**

    1. A sealed vault, 30 feet square, with walls of solid obsidian 3 feet thick. The single entrance is a copper door covered in glyph wards — twelve small sigils arranged in a circle around the Chained Sun. The door has no lock, no handle, and no visible mechanism. It opens only when the wards are disabled or when the Oracle's copper key is used
    2. Inside, the chamber is dark — the only space on Floor 3 not lit by the volcanic rift. The air is dry and still, preserved by the seals. Stone pedestals line the walls, each holding relics of Zanethi history: ritual implements, copper scrolls, consecrated weapons, and preserved offerings from centuries of worship
    3. At the chamber's center, on a raised obsidian dais, sits a brass reliquary-frame — an intricate mechanism of interlocking brass rings, copper wire, and crystal mounts. Suspended in the frame's center, pulsing with a deep amber glow: the Hunger for Justice. The dream-gem
    4. The gem's presence is immediate. Standing in the room, every creature feels the weight of every wrong it has ever committed — and every wrong committed against it. Grudges surface. Old wounds reopen. The sense that *someone must pay* is overwhelming

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **The Ward Door**: Twelve glyph wards on the door. Using the Oracle's copper key deactivates all twelve simultaneously. Without the key:
        1. Each ward must be individually disabled (Disable Device DC 24, 1 round per ward)
        2. Failing a Disable Device check by 5 or more triggers the failed ward: random effect per ward (roll 1d4: 1 = 3d8 fire, Reflex DC 16 half; 2 = Bestow Curse, Will DC 16; 3 = Hold Person, Will DC 16; 4 = 4d6 sonic, Fort DC 16 or deafened 1 hour)
        3. Dispel Magic can suppress a ward temporarily (CL check DC 18 per ward, suppressed for 1d4+1 rounds)
        4. Brute force: the door has Hardness 12, 80 hp, and triggering all remaining wards on the door simultaneously if destroyed by damage
    2. **The Reliquary-Frame**: The dream-gem is suspended in the brass mechanism. Removing it requires either:
        1. _Carefully disassembling the frame_ (Disable Device DC 20 or Craft (metalworking) DC 18, 10 rounds). Safe extraction. The frame is worth 1,500 gp as a precision instrument
        2. _Breaking the frame_ (Hardness 8, 30 hp). The gem is not damaged (dream-gems are indestructible) but the frame's destruction sends a pulse of prophetic energy: all creatures in the room make Will DC 18 or are stunned for 1d4 rounds as they experience a flood of visions of cosmic injustice
        3. _If the Oracle disabled the wards and suppressed the guardian_: the frame's mechanism unlocks on its own, presenting the gem for easy removal. No check required
    3. **Dream-Gem Ambient Effect**: While in the same room as the Hunger for Justice, all creatures are affected:
        1. Will DC 14 each stretch (or each minute during combat) or compelled to confess a wrong they have committed — the confession is verbal, involuntary, and emotionally charged. This is a mind-affecting compulsion effect. Creatures immune to compulsion are unaffected
        2. All creatures in the room take -2 on saves against Enchantment effects (the gem erodes mental defenses by making every thought feel like a judgment)
        3. A creature that picks up the gem must make an immediate Will save (DC 18) or be consumed by a vision of the "justice" the gem encodes — a world where every wrong is answered, every debt collected, every slight repaid. The vision lasts 1 round (the creature is stunned) and then fades, leaving the bearer with the gem's passive properties (see dungeon Introduction)
    4. **Floor Traps**: Three pressure plates in the room (Search DC 22 each):
        1. _Plate 1_ (between the door and the first pedestal): triggers a _Flame Strike_ from a ceiling glyph (10d6, half fire/half divine, Reflex DC 18 half, 10-foot radius)
        2. _Plate 2_ (in front of the dais): triggers a _Hold Person_ on the creature that stepped on it (Will DC 18, duration 7 rounds)
        3. _Plate 3_ (directly beneath the reliquary-frame): triggers only if weight is removed from the frame (the gem is taken). Releases the construct guardian if it has not already been activated

3. **Encounters**

    1. 1 **Brass Construct Guardian** (use Iron Golem, MM pg. 136, with the following modifications: reduce HD to 10, hp to 80, CR to 10. Replace poison gas breath with superheated brass vapor breath: 10-foot cube, 2d6 fire + 1d4 Constitution damage, Fortitude DC 17 negates Con damage, usable every 1d4+1 rounds. Fire immunity. Construct traits. DR 10/adamantine) — inert in an alcove near the dais, disguised as a decorative brass statue. It activates when the gem is removed from the frame (pressure plate 3) or when any creature touches the dais without speaking the prayer of passage. If the Oracle suppressed it, it remains inert for 1 hour
        1. _Tactics_: The guardian fights to recover the gem and return it to the frame. It prioritizes the creature carrying the gem. It does not pursue beyond the Reliquary's door. It uses its breath weapon to block the exit and its slam attacks (+14 melee, 2d10+9) to pin targets against the walls. It cannot speak, does not negotiate, and is immune to mind-affecting effects

4. **Treasure**

    1. **The Hunger for Justice** (dream-gem) — the dungeon's primary prize. See the dungeon Introduction for full properties. The gem is a fist-sized crystal of deep amber, warm to the touch, pulsing with light in a rhythm that matches the bearer's heartbeat
    2. The reliquary-frame (if recovered intact): worth 1,500 gp as a precision divination instrument. Can be repurposed for other scrying applications (Craft (metalworking) DC 18 and a _Scrying_ spell component)
    3. The pedestal relics (8 pedestals):
        1. A copper-bound tome of Zanethi liturgy (worth 200 gp to a collector)
        2. A consecrated falchion — _+1 Flaming Falchion_ (DMG pg. 224, worth 8,315 gp), the personal weapon of a Flame-Taken champion from a century ago
        3. A preserved hand in a copper reliquary, wearing a _Ring of Counterspells_ (DMG pg. 230, worth 4,000 gp, currently storing Dispel Magic)
        4. An obsidian mask with copper eye-sockets — _Eyes of the Eagle_ (DMG pg. 256, worth 2,500 gp), used by temple sentries
        5. Zanethi coinage: 1,200 gp in ancient brass and copper coins (collector value: 1,800 gp due to age)
        6. A sealed copper scroll case containing a _Scroll of Greater Dispel Magic_ (CL 11, worth 1,650 gp) and a _Scroll of True Seeing_ (CL 9, worth 1,375 gp)
        7. A brass orrery depicting the cosmology of Zahr al-Naras (worth 3,000 gp to Myrian scholars or anyone studying the Celestine Furnace)
        8. A _Cloak of Resistance +2_ (DMG pg. 253, worth 4,000 gp), folded neatly with a note pinned to it in Ignan: "For whoever comes after me"
