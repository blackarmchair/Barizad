1. **Description**

    1. A cathedral-scale chamber carved from volcanic glass — 80 feet long, 40 feet wide, 50 feet to the vaulted ceiling. Every surface is polished obsidian, reflecting the volcanic glow from below into a disorienting infinity of red-orange light. The reflections show the chamber as it is — and as it is not. Figures move in the glass that have no counterpart in the room
    2. The floor is a mosaic of obsidian sections — some stable, some cracked and thin over the volcanic rift below. The stable sections are marked with brass inlay (the paths worn smooth by centuries of priestly traffic). The unstable sections look identical to the naked eye
    3. Four Flame-Taken veterans stand at attention along the nave's central axis, two on each side, facing inward. They wear full plate of copper-wrought design, their faces hidden behind featureless brass masks. They do not move, do not speak, and do not react to the party's entrance — until the party crosses the chamber's midpoint
    4. At the far end, a massive obsidian arch frames the corridor leading deeper into Floor 3. The arch is carved with the Chained Sun, 10 feet across, and the chains in the carving appear to move in the reflected light — a trick of the volcanic glow, or something else

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **Unstable Floor**: The obsidian floor is divided into approximately 30 sections (5-foot squares). Eight of these are unstable — cracked thin over a 40-foot drop into a subsidiary rift channel (not the main rift, but hot enough: 4d6 fire damage at the bottom, plus 4d6 falling damage). Spot DC 18 to notice hairline cracks differentiating unstable from stable sections. Knowledge (architecture/engineering) or Knowledge (nature) DC 16 to identify the pattern (unstable sections cluster near the chamber's edges). Stepping on an unstable section: Reflex DC 16 or the section collapses, dropping the creature 40 feet. A creature that succeeds catches the edge and can pull itself up (move action)
    2. **The Reflections**: The obsidian walls show reflections that are *almost* accurate. Intermittently (every 1d4 rounds during combat, or every stretch during exploration), a reflection shows something different: a party member's double making a different choice, a figure standing where no one stands, a door in a wall that has no door. These are not illusions — they cannot be dispelled, detected as magic, or interacted with. They are prophetic residue. A Knowledge (arcana) or Knowledge (religion) DC 16 check identifies them as "temporal echoes" — reflections of possible futures bleeding through the obsidian. One specific reflection (Spot DC 20 to notice) shows the party standing in the Pyre Ascendant with Naehri dead at their feet — foreshadowing that is *not* guaranteed to come true
    3. **The Brass Path**: The brass-inlaid paths on the floor mark safe routes. Following them avoids all unstable sections. The paths form a specific pattern — the Chained Sun glyph, visible only from above. A creature with fly or a similar vantage point automatically identifies the safe path
    4. **The Midpoint Trigger**: The veterans activate when any creature crosses the chamber's midpoint (40 feet from either entrance). This is not a physical trigger — the veterans are watching through their masks' eye slits. A successful Move Silently check (DC 20, opposed by Listen +8) allows a creature to cross without triggering the response, but the veterans attack the moment any hostile action is taken regardless

3. **Encounters**

    1. 4 **Flame-Taken Veterans** (Fighter 5, CR 5 each) — elite temple guards in copper full plate (AC 20). They fight in perfect coordination: two engage in melee (masterwork falchions, +10 melee, 2d4+5, 18-20/x2) while two use composite longbows (+8 ranged, 1d8+3) from 30 feet back. They rotate: when a melee veteran drops below half hp, a ranged veteran moves forward and the wounded one falls back. They use the brass paths instinctively (never step on unstable sections) and attempt to bull rush enemies onto unstable floor
    2. If the party entered Floor 3 without being detected (came through the Fever Cells' service passage or used stealth in the Crucible Stair), only 2 veterans are present — the other 2 are on patrol and arrive in 1d4+2 rounds
