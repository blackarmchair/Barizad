1. **Description**

    1. The Temple's main entrance is a gatehouse carved from a single block of black obsidian, flanked by two squat towers. Fire gutters run down the face of the gate like tears of burning oil, filling the air with the reek of sacred tallow
    2. A sacrificial trench cuts across the approach — 10 feet wide, 5 feet deep, filled with ash and charred bones. A narrow brass bridge spans it, retracted flush against the far wall by a chain mechanism
    3. Beyond the trench, the gate itself is a 15-foot archway sealed with copper-banded doors bearing the Chained Sun sigil. The doors stand ajar — the Temple does not fear visitors, it consumes them
    4. Two Azer sentries stand in alcoves flanking the gate, their bodies radiating waves of heat. A third watches from the left tower's arrow slit. The air tastes of sulfur and burnt fat

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **The Sacrificial Trench**: 10 feet wide, 5 feet deep. Jump DC 12 to clear with a running start, DC 20 from standing. Climbing down and up costs a move action each (no check required). The trench contains 2 feet of loose ash — any creature moving through it at more than half speed must make a Balance check (DC 12) or fall prone, sending up a cloud of ash (concealment in a 10-foot radius for 1 round)
    2. **The Brass Bridge**: Retracted against the far wall. The chain mechanism (Search DC 15 to locate on the near side) can be operated with a full-round action and a Strength check (DC 14). Alternatively, a Use Rope check (DC 18) with a grappling hook can snag the bridge and pull it across. The mechanism makes noise — Azer sentries get a Listen check (DC 5 due to proximity) to detect it
    3. **The Fire Gutters**: Channels of burning oil running down the gate's face and along corridor walls. Contact deals 1d6 fire damage. The oil is sacred — extinguishing it (by any means) triggers immediate hostility from any Aram-Zaneth within line of sight, regardless of prior disposition
    4. **The Tower**: The left tower is accessible via a ladder (Climb DC 5) from the far side of the trench. The tower sentry has line of sight to the approach (120 feet) and the courtyard beyond. If alerted, the sentry signals with a horn — the Courtyard of Ashes is then forewarned (no surprise round possible)
    5. **Stealth Approach**: The party can approach through a drainage culvert beneath the trench (Search DC 18 to notice, Escape Artist DC 15 or Squeeze check to fit through — Small creatures pass freely). This bypasses the trench and the sentries but deposits the party inside the gate with no cover

3. **Encounters**

    1. 3 **Azer** (MM pg. 21, CR 2 each) — two flanking the gate in alcoves, one in the tower. They fight in formation: the tower sentry sounds the horn on its first turn while the gate pair advance with warhammers, using their heat aura (1d6 fire to creatures grappling or struck by natural weapons) to punish melee. They do not pursue beyond the gate
    2. If the horn sounds, 2 additional **Flame-Taken Warriors** (Fighter 2, use Warrior stats from DMG pg. 117 with Improved Initiative and Power Attack) arrive from the Courtyard in 1d4+1 rounds. They wear copper-wrought chainmail and carry falchions branded with Zanethi glyphs

4. **Treasure**

    1. The tower contains: a signal horn of brass and bone (worth 25 gp), a _Potion of Cure Moderate Wounds_, and a leather satchel with 140 gp in mixed Zanethi coinage (triangular copper coins stamped with the Chained Sun)
    2. Each Azer carries: a masterwork warhammer (312 gp each) and 2d10 gp in brass trade-tokens
    3. The chain mechanism housing (Search DC 20): a _Scroll of Resist Energy (Fire)_ (CL 3, worth 150 gp), left by a previous infiltrator
