1. **Description**

    1. A long, low-ceilinged communal hall — 60 feet long, 30 feet wide — where the Temple's acolytes eat, pray, and inhale sacred incense between shifts. Stone benches line both walls. The air is dense with sweet, narcotic smoke pouring from copper censers suspended on chains every 10 feet
    2. Thirty or more acolytes sit or recline on the benches in various states of trance. Some murmur prayers, eyes rolled back. Others eat from brass bowls with mechanical indifference. Their robes are ash-grey, their foreheads marked with the Chained Sun in dried blood
    3. A serving station at the hall's north end dispenses a thick, dark broth from a copper cauldron. The broth smells of charcoal and something metallic — brass shavings, ground fine. Behind the serving station, a section of wall is subtly different: newer stonework, slightly recessed
    4. The room pulses with low chanting — not coordinated, but overlapping, creating a dissonant hum that vibrates in the chest. The smoke softens all edges, making distances uncertain and faces indistinct

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **Narcotic Incense**: At the end of every stretch spent in the Smoke Refectory, each creature must make a Fortitude save (DC 14) or suffer the effects of narcotic inhalation:
        1. _First failed save_: Dazed for 1d4 rounds. The creature perceives the acolytes' chanting as intelligible speech — fragments of prophecy, accusations, secrets. None of it is real
        2. _Second failed save_: Fascinated for 1d4 minutes. The creature sees visions in the smoke — burning cities, chained suns, a figure wreathed in fire speaking words that feel important but cannot be remembered afterward
        3. _Third failed save_: Confused for 1d4 rounds (as the spell). The creature cannot distinguish the visions from reality
    2. **Negate the Incense**: Delay Poison or Neutralize Poison suppresses the effects. A wet cloth over the mouth grants +4 on the save. The censers can be extinguished (each requires a standard action and a Strength check DC 10 to pull down, then smothered), but doing so alerts the acolytes and makes them hostile. Casting wind-based spells (Gust of Wind, etc.) clears a 20-foot area for 1d4 rounds
    3. **The Acolytes**: 30+ Aram-Zaneth acolytes (Adept 1, non-combatant). They are not hostile unless provoked. "Provocation" includes: extinguishing censers, open violence, defacing the Chained Sun symbol, or speaking Zaneth's name without the proper honorific ("He Who Was Bound"). If provoked, they mob — individually weak but 30 bodies in a cramped hall is a serious obstacle. Treat as a mob: attack +2 melee (improvised weapons, 1d4), AC 10, hp 150 total, disperses at 75 hp. Killing acolytes is narratively significant — these are civilians
    4. **The Hidden Passage**: Behind the serving station. Search DC 20 to notice the newer stonework. Once found, a Disable Device check (DC 15) or Strength check (DC 20) opens the concealed door. The passage leads to a narrow stair descending directly to Floor 2 (the Hall of Woven Smoke), bypassing the Branding Hall and the Throat of Cinders entirely. If the party has rescued Faris from the Courtyard, he can describe this passage (granting +4 on the Search check)
    5. **The Broth**: Consuming the broth grants fire resistance 5 for 1 hour but imposes -2 on Will saves for the same duration. The acolytes drink it constantly. An Alchemy or Heal check (DC 16) identifies the brass shavings and their mild narcotic properties

3. **Encounters**

    1. 30+ **Aram-Zaneth Acolytes** (Adept 1, non-combatant unless provoked) — see Mechanics for mob rules. They ignore the party as long as the party does not provoke them. An acolyte in a deep trance (Spot DC 14 to identify) clutches a _Scroll of Remove Curse_ — she stole it from the Branding Hall and is trying to undo her own initiation
    2. 1 **Incense Keeper** (Adept 4, CR 3) — tends the censers. An old woman with brass-capped fingers and blind, milky eyes. She can sense creatures by their body heat (blindsense 15 ft.) due to years of incense exposure. She is not hostile but will report intruders to the Branding Hall if she detects them. Can be bribed (Diplomacy DC 18 or 50 gp) to "not notice" the party, or she can be avoided entirely (Move Silently DC 14 to pass her without triggering her blindsense)
    3. If combat erupts in the Courtyard and the alarm was raised, 2 **Flame-Taken Berserkers** (Barbarian 2, as Courtyard) are in the refectory, resting. They wake and rage in 1d3 rounds

4. **Treasure**

    1. The Incense Keeper's station (Search DC 18): 3 doses of refined sacred incense (worth 50 gp each — can be used as alchemical smoke, granting concealment in a 10-foot radius for 3 rounds, or sold to collectors of religious artifacts)
    2. The acolyte with the scroll: _Scroll of Remove Curse_ (CL 5, worth 375 gp). She will give it freely if the party promises to help her escape the Temple (a quest hook for later)
    3. Behind the serving station (found automatically when discovering the hidden passage): a brass coffer containing 85 gp, a _Potion of Owl's Wisdom_, and a tarnished silver holy symbol of a pre-Zanethi fire cult (worth 120 gp to a historian, Knowledge (religion) DC 16 to identify its significance — it predates the Aram-Zaneth by centuries)
