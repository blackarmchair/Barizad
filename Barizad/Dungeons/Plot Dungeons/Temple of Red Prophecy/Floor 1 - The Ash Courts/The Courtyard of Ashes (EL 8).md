1. **Description**

    1. An open-air ritual courtyard, 80 feet across, paved with compressed volcanic ash and ringed by obsidian pillars carved to resemble chained figures writhing in flame. The sky above is hazy with smoke from the volcanic rift below
    2. At the center, a bonfire altar — a 10-foot-diameter brass bowl on a raised obsidian platform, filled with burning coals and offerings. Chained to a copper post beside the altar is a bound figure: a prisoner in torn robes, gagged with ash-soaked cloth, skin marked with preliminary branding cuts
    3. Flame-Taken berserkers drill in a loose formation on the courtyard's south side — six warriors performing ritual combat forms with falchions, chanting in low Ignan. Their bodies are latticed with burns and ash-inked runes
    4. Volcanic vents line the courtyard's eastern edge — capped brass pipes that periodically belch gouts of superheated steam. The ground near them is discolored and cracked

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **Volcanic Vents**: Every 3 stretches (or every 10 rounds during combat), 1d3 vents erupt. Each vent affects a 10-foot radius: Reflex DC 15 or take 3d6 fire damage (half on success). The vents hiss audibly 1 round before erupting (Listen DC 10 to notice and move). A character studying the pattern for 1 stretch can predict which vents will fire next (Knowledge (nature) or Survival DC 16)
    2. **The Bonfire Altar**: The coals deal 2d6 fire damage per round to anything in the bowl. The brass bowl itself is hot to the touch (1d4 fire damage to bare skin). The altar platform is 3 feet high (no movement penalty to step up). Sacred offerings in the coals include carved bone tokens, strips of skin with prayers written in blood, and copper rings — disturbing the offerings triggers hostility from all Aram-Zaneth in the courtyard
    3. **The Bound Prisoner**: A Nisarun merchant (non-combatant, Commoner 2) named Faris, captured for trading in "prophecy-contraband" (unauthorized dream-gem fragments — actually worthless glass). Freeing Faris requires cutting the copper chain (Hardness 10, 15 hp) or picking the lock (Open Lock DC 18). Faris knows the layout of the Ash Courts and can describe the Smoke Refectory's hidden passage (granting +4 on the Search check to find it) if rescued
    4. **Moral Complication**: Faris is scheduled for sacrifice at the next sunset. If the party intervenes, every Flame-Taken in the courtyard attacks immediately. If the party delays, Faris can be rescued during a lull — but only before the party leaves Floor 1. Abandoning Faris has no mechanical consequence but the DM should note it for future roleplay
    5. **Obsidian Pillars**: The carved pillars provide cover (+4 AC) and can be used as chokepoints. Each pillar is 3 feet in diameter and 12 feet tall. A Strength check (DC 22) can topple a pillar onto an adjacent creature (4d6 bludgeoning, Reflex DC 16 to avoid)

3. **Encounters**

    1. 4 **Flame-Taken Berserkers** (Barbarian 2, CR 2 each) — drilling in formation. They rage on their first round of combat and fight recklessly, using Power Attack (-2/+4). In rage: AC 12, hp 28 each, attack +7 (falchion, 2d4+8, 18-20/x2). Two additional berserkers are off-duty in the Smoke Refectory and arrive in 1d4+2 rounds if combat lasts
    2. 1 **Flame-Taken Drill Sergeant** (Fighter 4, CR 4) — commands the berserkers. Wears masterwork copper breastplate (AC 18). Fights intelligently: uses Combat Expertise and trips with a guisarme (+9 melee, 2d4+5, trip +7). If the fight turns, he retreats toward the Branding Hall to raise the alarm

4. **Treasure**

    1. The bonfire altar contains (Search DC 16, requires Resist Energy or similar protection to sift through): 220 gp in melted copper rings, a _Brooch of Shielding_ (DMG pg. 256, 25 charges remaining, worth ~750 gp) partially fused into the coals, and an ornate sacrificial dagger of obsidian and brass (masterwork dagger, worth 302 gp, holy symbol of Zaneth)
    2. The Drill Sergeant carries: a masterwork guisarme (308 gp), a _Potion of Bull's Strength_, and a brass key that opens the inner gate to the Branding Hall
    3. Faris, if rescued, offers the party 200 gp worth of trade goods cached in the city (redeemable later) and information about Aram-Zaneth patrol routes in Ash-Zurab
