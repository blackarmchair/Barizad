1. **Description**

    1. A rectangular chamber, 40 feet by 50 feet, with iron-reinforced walls and a ceiling hung with chains, hooks, and copper-framed brands glowing cherry-red in cradle-forges. This is where initiates receive the ritual scarification that marks them as Flame-Taken — and where heretics are branded for their transgressions
    2. An iron-barred inner gate dominates the far wall, leading deeper into the Temple toward the Throat of Cinders. The gate is locked. Beside it hangs a rack of ritual implements: branding irons shaped like Zanethi glyphs, curved flaying knives, copper clamps, and a basin of caustic alchemical paste used to set brands permanently
    3. The walls are covered in carved bas-reliefs — a visual history of Zaneth's "exile" as the Aram-Zaneth tell it. The carvings show: a radiant figure defying a mechanical heaven, chains of brass descending, a burning fall, and the figure landing among mortals with arms outstretched. It is beautiful, heroic, and — as the Archive of Heresies on Floor 2 will reveal — significantly distorted
    4. A stone slab in the center of the room serves as the branding table. Leather restraints hang from its sides. The slab is stained dark with old blood and the chemical residue of the caustic paste. The air smells of burnt flesh and copper

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **The Inner Gate**: Locked (Open Lock DC 22) and barred from the far side (Break DC 25, Hardness 10, hp 60). The brass key carried by the Drill Sergeant in the Courtyard opens it. Alternatively, the Flame-Taken Sergeant in this room carries a key
    2. **Ritual Implements as Weapons**: The branding irons function as light maces that deal an additional 1d4 fire damage on a hit (heated). The flaying knives are masterwork daggers. The caustic paste deals 1d6 acid damage on contact and 1d4 acid damage per round for 1d3 rounds (Fortitude DC 14 negates the ongoing damage). There are enough implements for the party to arm themselves if needed
    3. **The Wall Carvings**: Decipher Script DC 18 or Comprehend Languages to read the Ignan inscriptions. The carvings depict Zaneth's exile as a heroic sacrifice: he chose to fall so mortals could be free. A Knowledge (religion) DC 20 check recognizes subtle inconsistencies — the carvings omit the Celestine Furnace entirely, presenting the rebellion as purely theological rather than involving the machinery of prophecy. This foreshadows the revelations on Floor 2
    4. **The Branding Table**: A creature restrained on the table is pinned (as grapple). The restraints can be broken (Strength DC 18) or picked (Open Lock DC 16). An initiate is currently strapped to the table, mid-branding — an unconscious Flame-Taken recruit (Warrior 1, hp 8, currently at 2 hp, stabilized). If revived, the recruit is disoriented and frightened but loyal to the Temple. She will not help the party but will not fight them either. If the party heals her, she may (Diplomacy DC 22) reveal that "the sergeant answers to the ones below — the ones who drink smoke and dream for the Oracle"

3. **Encounters**

    1. 1 **Flame-Taken Sergeant** (Fighter 6, CR 6) — a scarred veteran with geometric burn patterns covering both arms. She wears _+1 copper breastplate_ (AC 20), fights with a masterwork falchion (+11/+6 melee, 2d4+6, 18-20/x2) and uses Improved Trip (+10) to drop targets onto the branding table (if adjacent). She fights to the death — retreating would dishonor her brands
    2. 2 **Flame-Taken Warriors** (Fighter 3, CR 3 each) — mid-initiation assistants. They wear chain shirts (AC 16) and fight with branding irons (light mace +6 melee, 1d6+3 plus 1d4 fire). They flank with the sergeant and use Aid Another to boost her attacks if outmatched
    3. 1 **Azer Smithy** (MM pg. 21, CR 2) — maintains the cradle-forges. Fights only if the forges are threatened or extinguished. Otherwise attempts to flee toward the Throat of Cinders to warn Thorrak

4. **Treasure**

    1. The sergeant carries: _+1 copper breastplate_ (worth 1,350 gp — functions as a breastplate with a copper-red finish and Zanethi glyphs, no special properties beyond the enhancement), a masterwork falchion (375 gp), a brass key to the inner gate, and a leather pouch containing 180 gp and a flawed fire opal (worth 250 gp)
    2. The rack of ritual implements: 6 branding irons (5 gp each as curios), 3 masterwork daggers (302 gp each), and 2 doses of caustic paste (50 gp each, alchemical item)
    3. Concealed in the branding table's base (Search DC 22): a hidden compartment containing a _Wand of Cure Light Wounds_ (18 charges, worth 270 gp) — hidden by a previous initiate who could not endure the branding and planned to escape. She never did
