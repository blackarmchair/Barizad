## Purpose

- Establish the Temple as alien, hostile, and actively defended — this is not a ruin but a functioning place of worship
- Introduce Aram-Zaneth aesthetics and theology through environmental storytelling: fire gutters, sacrificial trenches, branded warriors, obsidian architecture
- Present the party with moral complications alongside combat — a bound sacrifice, non-combatant acolytes, the question of how much violence is justified in someone else's sacred space
- Foreshadow the Thirsting Light as serious rivals with the Thorrak encounter

## Themes

- Overwhelming sensory assault — heat, smoke, chanting, the smell of burnt offerings
- Sacred violence — the Aram-Zaneth do not distinguish between worship and warfare
- A functioning theocracy seen from the inside — drill yards, communal halls, initiation chambers
- The volcanic rift as living geography — vents, trenches, and cinder-choked descents

## Tone

Combat-forward with environmental complications. The Ash Courts are the public face of the Temple — loud, hot, dangerous, and full of believers. The party is trespassing in a place that is actively defended by people who consider violence a sacrament. Every room demands a decision: fight, sneak, or talk — and each choice has consequences that echo through the rest of the dungeon.

## Level Range

- EL 7–9
- Designed for a party of four at levels 8–9

## Structure

Five chambers arranged around the volcanic rift's upper terrace. The party enters through the Gate and must reach the Throat of Cinders to descend to Floor 2. The Smoke Refectory offers an alternative path that bypasses the Branding Hall if the party avoids violence.

1. The Gate of Burning Testimony (EL 7)
2. The Courtyard of Ashes (EL 8)
3. The Smoke Refectory (EL 8)
4. The Branding Hall (EL 9)
5. The Throat of Cinders (EL 8)
