1. **Description**

    1. A vertical shaft dropping 150 feet into the volcanic rift — the throat of the mountain itself. The walls are rough obsidian veined with dull copper that pulses with heat. The shaft is 15 feet in diameter, narrowing to 5 feet at a choke point 80 feet down before widening again
    2. Iron pitons and a frayed rope ladder are bolted to the near wall — the official descent route, maintained poorly. Chunks of obsidian have broken away, leaving gaps in the handholds. Far below, volcanic glow paints everything in deep orange
    3. At the 80-foot mark, where the shaft narrows, a natural ledge juts from the wall — 10 feet wide, 15 feet long, partially screened by a curtain of volcanic gas. Something large is coiled on the ledge. The gas smells of sulfur and rot
    4. The shaft opens at the bottom into a transition corridor leading to Floor 2. The heat increases steadily with depth — by the bottom, metal armor is uncomfortable to touch and exposed skin prickles

2. **Mechanics**

    1. **The Descent**: Climb DC 18 using the wall (rough obsidian provides handholds). The rope ladder reduces this to Climb DC 8 but the frayed sections require a Climb check (DC 12) at 40 feet and 100 feet — failure means the rope breaks and the climber falls from that point. Pitons and proper rope (Use Rope DC 15 to secure) reduce the check to DC 5 with no risk of rope failure
    2. **The Choke Point**: At 80 feet, the shaft narrows to 5 feet. Medium creatures must squeeze (half speed, -4 to attack rolls and AC) for 10 feet. Large creatures cannot pass without widening the gap (Stone Shape, or Strength DC 26 to break obsidian, dealing 2d6 damage to the creature from sharp edges). This is where Thorrak has positioned himself
    3. **Heat Escalation**: The shaft gets hotter with depth. Above 80 feet: no effect. Below 80 feet: Fortitude DC 12 every 10 minutes or take 1d4 nonlethal damage (as severe heat, DMG pg. 303). Below 120 feet: Fortitude DC 15 every 10 minutes, and metal armor or weapons deal 1 point of fire damage per round to the bearer until cooled
    4. **Volcanic Gas**: The gas curtain at the ledge provides concealment (20% miss chance). Breathing the gas requires a Fortitude save (DC 14) each round or the creature is nauseated for 1 round. Holding one's breath negates this. Wind effects disperse the gas for 1d4 rounds
    5. **Falling**: A creature that falls takes 1d6 damage per 10 feet fallen (maximum 15d6 for the full 150 feet). The bottom of the shaft is rough stone — no water or soft landing

3. **Encounters**

    1. **Thorrak Rootbound, Grove-Spawn of Haran'Set** (Druid 9, CR 9) — see stat block at `Barizad/Stat Blocks/Rival Parties/The Thirsting Light/Thorrak Rootbound, Grove-Spawn of Haran'Set.md`. Thorrak waits on the ledge at the 80-foot mark, concealed by the volcanic gas and Wild Shaped into a Large Viper. His animal companion (a fire-scarred dire boar) waits at the bottom of the shaft
        1. _Tactics_: Thorrak lets the first climber reach the choke point, then strikes from the gas curtain — reverting to natural form to cast Flame Strike (centered on the choke point to catch climbers in a vertical column). He follows with Call Lightning and Spike Growth on the ledge to prevent the party from establishing a foothold. If the party reaches the ledge in force, he Wild Shapes into a Dire Wolf and fights alongside the gas curtain's concealment. His dire boar attacks anyone who reaches the bottom
        2. _Retreat_: If reduced below 20 hp, Thorrak casts Obscuring Mist and retreats down the shaft to Floor 2, warning Sister Yussa. If he escapes, he joins Naehri on Floor 3 for the final fight
        3. _Personality_: Thorrak speaks least of the Thirsting Light. He watches the party like a naturalist observing specimens. If spoken to, he says only: "The fire tests what deserves to grow. I am here to see if you are weeds or saplings." He is genuinely curious about the party's capabilities — this is reconnaissance for the Thirsting Light as much as it is a fight
    2. The **Dire Boar** companion (as MM pg. 63, but with fire-scarred appearance, blackened with fungal growths, 51 hp) guards the shaft's base. It attacks anything that reaches the bottom while Thorrak fights above, creating a pincer. If Thorrak falls, the boar fights to the death

The ledge itself (Search DC 16): Thorrak's camp — a bedroll of woven fungal fibers, 3 days of rations (dried mushrooms and smoked meat), a _Potion of Barkskin_ (CL 6, +3 natural armor), and a leather journal written in Druidic detailing his observations of the Temple's ecosystem. The journal mentions that "the smoke-drinkers on the second level see things that are not there — or perhaps things that are not there *yet*"
