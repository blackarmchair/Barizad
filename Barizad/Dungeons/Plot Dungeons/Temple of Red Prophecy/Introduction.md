## Purpose

- The central shrine of the Aram-Zaneth theocracy, located in Ash-Zurab — a capital city built into a volcanic rift
- A raid dungeon: the party needs something inside (a dream-gem, intel on Zaneth's true nature, or the High Oracle's signet)
- Introduces the Aram-Zaneth as a living, functioning theocracy rather than a ruin — the danger comes from the fact that everything here is *working as intended*
- The Thirsting Light (a CR 9 rival party) operates throughout the dungeon as boss encounters on each floor
- Provides the party's first sustained encounter with Zanethi theology, allowing them to discover that the Aram-Zaneth have never actually communicated with Zaneth

## Themes

- Volcanic sacred architecture — obsidian ziggurats, fire gutters, sacrificial trenches, molten copper veins
- Ecstatic religion as hazard — incense that induces visions, chanting that imposes Will saves, ritual scarification tools
- Prophecy as environmental threat — rooms where visions manifest physically, dream-logic puzzles
- Ascending initiation — each floor goes deeper into the cult's secrets and closer to the truth about Zaneth

## Tone

Where Fallen Zahr al-Naras is an industrial ruin — broken machines, residual heat, frozen time — the Temple of Red Prophecy is a living hostile shrine. Active worship, fanatical defenders, and prophetic phenomena that warp perception. The dungeon is dangerous not because it's broken, but because it's working exactly as intended. Each floor escalates from physical hostility (Floor 1) through perceptual disorientation (Floor 2) to cosmic dread (Floor 3).

## Level Range

- EL 7–12
- Designed for a party of four at levels 8–10

## Structure

Three floors descending into the volcanic rift beneath the Temple. Each floor represents a deeper level of Zanethi initiation.

1. **Floor 1 — The Ash Courts** (EL 7–9): The public-facing temple grounds. Combat-forward with environmental complications. Five rooms.
2. **Floor 2 — The Crucible of Visions** (EL 9–10): The inner sanctum. Prophetic visions intrude on reality. Coordination puzzles replace straightforward combat. Five rooms.
3. **Floor 3 — The Oracle's Fundament** (EL 10–12): The climax. The High Oracle, the dream-gem, and Naehri's final stand. Five rooms.

## The Thirsting Light

The Thirsting Light is a four-person strike cell empowered by the Temple. Their members are distributed across the dungeon:

| Member | CR | Floor | Room |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Thorrak Rootbound | ~9 | 1 | The Throat of Cinders |
| Sister Yussa | 9 | 2 | The Bloodletting Nave |
| Vokhul the Devoured | 9 | 2 | The Crucible Stair |
| Naehri of the Thron-Dream | 9 | 3 | The Pyre Ascendant |

If the party doesn't kill a Thirsting Light member on a lower floor, that member retreats to Naehri's position for the final fight.

## The Dream-Gem: The Hunger for Justice

"Reckoning for all wrongs." The Aram-Zaneth recovered this gem and brought it to the Temple, where the High Oracle uses it to fuel her visions of cosmic injustice. Its ambient effects bleed into Floor 3: creatures near it feel compelled to confess wrongs, old grudges surface unbidden, and the sense that *someone must pay* pervades every interaction.

**Bearer properties (if taken):** The bearer gains an instinct for guilt — they can sense when someone nearby is concealing a wrong they've committed (as *zone of truth* but passive and emotional, not verbal). Drawback: the bearer becomes increasingly unable to forgive or let go. Every slight accumulates. Every compromise feels like complicity.

**Location:** The Reliquary on Floor 3, mounted in a brass reliquary-frame connected to the Oracle's divination apparatus.
