# The Singing Sands — Crawl Guide

## General Features

**Construction Material.** Sandstone walls, worn smooth by centuries of wind. Floors are flagstone where constructed, loose sand elsewhere. The False Desert is open sky and dunes — no walls or ceiling.

**Ceiling Height.** 15 feet in constructed rooms. Open sky in the False Desert. The Sphinx's Lair is a vast echoing chamber — 40 feet or more.

**Lighting.** The Entryway has two torches (likely magical — they never go out) providing bright light. The False Desert has a blazing, unmoving sun — bright light everywhere. The Gate room and Sphinx's Lair are unlit unless the party brings light.

## Crawl Strategy Notes

**Marching Order.** The Entryway and Gate room are open enough for double-file. The False Desert has no corridors — the party can spread out freely, but doing so risks separation during Whispers in the Dunes (Will DC 16 or run off alone).

**Visibility.** The False Desert's unmoving sun eliminates darkness concerns there. The Gate room and Sphinx's Lair are interior spaces with no natural light — Darkvision or light sources required.

**Spot/Listen.** Note each PC's Spot and Listen modifiers. Creatures with +5 or better in either are more likely to notice the flagstone pressure plates (Spot/Search DC 15) and hear the Sphinx before entering his chamber.

## Stretch Pacing

This dungeon has two distinct pacing modes: structured rooms (Entryway, Gate, Sphinx's Lair) and open traversal (the False Desert).

### Room-by-Room Estimates

| Area | Estimated Stretches | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Entryway | 1–2 | Riddle + investigation of crates. If the party overthinks the red herrings, it runs longer. |
| The False Desert | 6–18 | The desert demands hours of travel. Use stretches to pace the journey: 1 stretch = ~10 minutes of trudging through dunes. Roll for encounters every 6–12 stretches (see below). |
| Gate to Sphinx's Lair | 2–3 | Flagstone puzzle with trial-and-error. Each failed attempt triggers the lightning trap and resets. |
| Sphinx's Lair | 1–3 | Riddle encounter. If combat occurs, resolve it before returning to stretch tracking. |

### Relevant Activities (3.5e)

- **Search Area.** Search or Spot check to find hidden elements (pressure plates in the Gate room, the book's purpose in the Entryway).
- **Keep Watch.** Critical in the False Desert — the Whispers in the Dunes target isolated PCs. A watcher can alert the party and prevent surprise rounds.
- **Take Your Time.** A PC can Take 20 on the flagstone puzzle's Search check (DC 15) — costs 2 stretches but guarantees finding the pressure plates without stepping on them.
- **Move Quietly.** Move Silently checks in the False Desert may help avoid some encounters, at GM discretion.
- **Helping Out.** Aid Another on the Decipher Script check for the wall inscription in the Gate room.

## Wandering Monster Table — The False Desert

The False Desert is the only area large enough for wandering encounters. Replace the existing "every 1–2 hours" pacing with stretch-based timing.

**Encounter Frequency.** Hourly — roll 1d6 at the end of every 6th stretch spent in the False Desert.
**Encounter Likelihood.** Possible — on a 5 or 6, roll on the table below.
**Encounter Difficulty.** Challenging — these fights should drain resources but not kill outright.
